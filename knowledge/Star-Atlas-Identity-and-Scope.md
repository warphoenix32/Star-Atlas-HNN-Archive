# Star Atlas: Identity and Scope

Star Atlas is simultaneously a science-fiction setting, a family of games and interactive products, an on-chain economy, a developer platform, a player community, and a project built by institutions with different responsibilities. Those layers overlap, but they are not interchangeable. A spaceship described in lore is not automatically a released game asset; a roadmap is not a delivered feature; an asset listing is not proof of in-game utility; and a community vote is not the same thing as implementation by the organization responsible for carrying it out.

The clearest way to understand Star Atlas is to begin with those boundaries.

## A universe called Galia

The fictional setting is the [Galia Expanse](lore/Galia-Expanse.md), a region shaped by the histories of Manus Ultima Divina, the ONI Consortium, the Ustur, and many smaller peoples, corporations, governments, religious orders, and political movements. The Convergence War and the Council of Peace form part of the setting's institutional memory. Lore gives Star Atlas a world in which exploration, industry, conflict, diplomacy, trade, and identity can have narrative meaning beyond a single game mechanic.

The Archive treats the captured Star Atlas Lore Repository as the preferred taxonomy and nomenclature authority for the lore represented in that snapshot. Later official publications can extend the canon, while earlier sources remain historical evidence even when names or classifications change. See the [Canon Registry](lore/Canon-Registry.md).

## A family of experiences, not one immutable game

Star Atlas has reached players through several distinct surfaces. The [Product Registry](gameplay/Product-Registry.md) records these at the narrowest defensible level:

- the original Galactic Marketplace and its later replacement;
- SCORE, later commonly called Faction Fleet;
- the Unreal Engine Showroom and its named revisions;
- Escape Velocity, a time-bounded on-chain movement experiment;
- SAGE Labs and the Starbased update;
- Holosim and its chapter-based browser simulation;
- later test environments such as the C4 Public Test Realm.

These products do not share one lifecycle. Some were released and later superseded; some remain documented as accessible; some are tests; and some names describe product families whose individual builds have materially different states. The dated [official current-state snapshot](gameplay/Official-Current-State-Snapshot-2026-07-12.md) establishes which public pathways ATMTA presented in July 2026 without converting that snapshot into proof of uninterrupted availability.

## An economy built across products

ATLAS is the transferable token historically used in sales, rewards, marketplace activity, locking, gameplay costs, and treasury terms. POLIS is associated with governance, locking, and POLIS Voting Power. Neither token has one timeless function: its role changes by product and period. SCORE emissions, SAGE resource loops, marketplace trading, governance locking, and Holosim's simulated `zATLAS` belong to different systems and evidence states.

The economy therefore has to be read chronologically. An official economic report can establish what ATMTA measured or reported in a defined period; it does not independently audit the underlying activity. A governance authorization can establish permission to spend; it does not prove that payment occurred.

See [ATLAS Token History](economy/ATLAS-Token-History.md), [POLIS Token History](economy/POLIS-Token-History.md), and the [Economic Report Catalog](economy/Economic-Report-Catalog.md).

## The organizations behind the project

[ATMTA, Inc.](organizations/ATMTA.md) is the company associated with developing and publishing Star Atlas products. The Star Atlas DAO is the POLIS-holder governance body. The Star Atlas Foundation is described in the governance framework as a legal and administrative implementation body. The elected Star Atlas Council assists authors and performs delegated governance-process and program-administration functions.

These institutions must remain distinct. The Council is not an independent legislature; the Foundation is not the entire DAO; and ATMTA's product roadmap is not automatically a DAO commitment. The [Institutional Overview](organizations/Institutional-Overview.md) and [Governance and Economy Overview](governance/Governance-and-Economy-Overview.md) explain the evidence-supported division of roles.

## A community that records, builds, and contests meaning

Players have created guilds and DACs, economic tools, reporting outlets, broadcasts, guides, events, governance proposals, and persistent social histories. Atlas Brew, Town Halls, Aephia, Hologram News Network, community creators, and Discord discussions preserve much of the project's lived history.

Community evidence is valuable without being treated as automatically official. A guild's self-description is authoritative for how it presented itself; a contemporary report can preserve what participants understood at the time; and an unattributed transcript can establish that a subject was discussed at a specific timestamp. Stronger claims about corporate action, release state, identity, or payment still require appropriate evidence.

## What Star Atlas is as of this review

As of 2026-07-23, the repository supports describing Star Atlas as an actively documented multi-product science-fiction ecosystem with:

- a developed fictional setting and growing canon;
- browser, Unreal Engine, marketplace, and test-environment surfaces;
- Solana programs, tokens, assets, accounts, APIs, and developer tooling;
- formal POLIS-based governance institutions;
- a long-running player and creator community.

That description is deliberately broader than "a game" and narrower than every aspiration ever announced. It tells readers what kind of project Star Atlas is while preserving the difference between vision, documentation, testing, release, operation, and independently observed outcome.

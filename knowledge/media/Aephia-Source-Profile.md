# Aephia Industries Source Profile

## Summary

Aephia Industries is both a Star Atlas DAC and a long-running community publisher. Its website combines guild self-description, weekly news aggregation, gameplay guides, ship and collectible references, economic dashboards, and promotion of Aephia-built products.

The weekly newsletter is the highest-value archival series on the site. Aephia describes it as a Monday publication intended to aggregate newsworthy Star Atlas developments from the preceding week, including team revelations, product scheduling, governance, releases, and operational issues.

## Source classification

**Source class:** `[SRC-COMMUNITY] [SRC-GUILD] [SRC-MEDIA]`  
**Historical value:** `[REL-MAJOR]`  
**General reliability:** Medium to high for contemporaneous aggregation when claims are attributed and linked; lower for guild self-description, promotional claims, forecasts, and old operational guides.

## Editorial and institutional context

Aephia publicly describes itself as one of the first and largest Star Atlas DACs and states that its principal in-universe mission is to become a leading spacecraft manufacturer. It also describes an ambition to be player-governed, player-owned, and structured so value flows to participants rather than outside investors.

Because Aephia is an active guild and builder, its publications are not institutionally neutral. Coverage may reflect the interests of a major industrial DAC, its members, and its products. This does not negate the archival value of its weekly reporting, but guild affiliation should remain visible when Aephia reports on guild competition, governance, treasury allocation, or its own projects.

## Known publication lines

### Weekly Star Atlas Newsletter

- Numbered weekly series.
- Authored principally by Funcracker in the pages reviewed.
- Designed as a digest of the prior week's Star Atlas news.
- Often cites Atlas Brew, Discord statements, governance proposals, product releases, development discussion, and community projects.
- Reached at least issue #237 by July 6, 2026.

### Guides

The guide index includes current and historical explainers for:

- z.ink and its airdrop campaigns;
- AstralPass;
- Holosim;
- SAGE C4 regions;
- SLY Assistant and automation dashboards;
- infrastructure contracts;
- local markets;
- Aephia membership and guild identity.

Guides should be treated as time-bounded operational documentation. Interfaces, recipes, rewards, token plans, timelines, and access requirements may become stale quickly.

### Economic dashboards

Aephia states that it operates dashboards covering on-chain token developments, in-game activity, ship sales, and related economic indicators. Dashboard values require capture dates, methodology review, and preservation because live dashboards can change or disappear.

### Video relationship

Aephia identifies MetaVerse Explorer as its resident video creator and directs readers to that channel for Star Atlas development coverage and guides.

## Aephia projects and claims requiring separate verification

- AEP Life Form (ALF), described by Aephia as a Solana NFT and investment-club project.
- Cataclysm: Battle for Iris, described as a Star Atlas-based online strategy card game with an alpha and a physical adaptation for IMPACT.
- COPA 2024 guild award claim, described by Aephia as selection by Star Atlas team members as best DAC/guild.
- Aephia promotional claims concerning Discord activity, competition winners, awards, size, and community influence.

These statements are valid as Aephia's own claims but should not be presented as independently verified without supporting competition results, official announcements, archived metrics, or third-party records.

## Staleness policy

Aephia content is most reliable as a record of what was publicly known, expected, or discussed at publication time.

When ingesting an article:

1. Preserve the publication date prominently.
2. Distinguish reported releases from future plans and estimates.
3. Attribute statements relayed from named ATMTA personnel to the original speaker when possible.
4. Recheck time-sensitive claims against later official releases, proposals, repositories, or follow-up newsletters.
5. Never overwrite a historical claim merely because it later became outdated; record the later outcome as a correction, revision, delay, cancellation, or superseding event.
6. Treat guides as historical snapshots unless recently verified against the live product.

## Strengths

- Consistent numbered weekly chronology.
- Dense aggregation across official announcements and community venues.
- Frequent links to underlying sources.
- Strong coverage of SAGE systems, economy, governance, and development discussion.
- Useful preservation of Discord and broadcast statements that may be difficult to retrieve later.

## Risks and limitations

- Guild affiliation and self-promotional content.
- Secondary reporting of statements originally made in Discord, Atlas Brew, or other venues.
- Forecasts and proposed schedules can become outdated.
- Some summaries include interpretation or community speculation alongside factual reporting.
- Site templates repeat promotional and related-content blocks, which should be removed during ingestion.
- The full newsletter archive has not yet been enumerated through a complete sitemap or API export.

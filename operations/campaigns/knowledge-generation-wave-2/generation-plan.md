# Wave 2 Knowledge Generation Plan

## Decision

Authorize a broad but risk-tiered knowledge-generation campaign. Relax completeness requirements, not truth requirements. Pages may be incomplete, qualified, provisional, or explicitly historical, but every factual assertion must remain traceable and every material uncertainty must remain visible.

## Claim-level evidence requirements

Every material claim must record or resolve:

- exact source ID or repository path;
- source authority class;
- source date;
- subject entity;
- claim type;
- temporal scope;
- lifecycle state;
- corroboration status;
- contradiction status;
- attribution requirement;
- page risk classification.

## Page acceptance gates

A page may enter `knowledge/` when:

1. its identity and scope are unambiguous;
2. every material claim is cited;
3. lifecycle distinctions are preserved;
4. current-state claims are date-scoped;
5. contradictions are visible;
6. status metadata is present;
7. page risk is R1, R2, or approved R3;
8. no R5 claim is promoted;
9. all internal links resolve;
10. semantic review is complete before merge; drafting in a review-only implementation PR is permitted after the evidence-packet gate passes.

## Campaign success criteria

- 45–65 pages created or substantially expanded;
- at least 70% R1–R2;
- no uncited material claims;
- no proposal/result/execution collapse;
- no announcement/release collapse;
- no current-state claim without `as_of` scope;
- all provisional and historical pages visibly labeled;
- indexes and master timeline updated;
- no unresolved duplicate canonical entity pages;
- research gaps preserved rather than silently filled.

The **Garadar DAC** is a Decentralized Autonomous Corporation of heroic soldiers, intellectuals, and entrepreneurs that rose from a small Ustur monastic settlement to become the most notorious DAC in the galaxy. Born from Ustur contemplation, scarred by [Crumon](../factions/crumon_dynasty.md) exploitation, and forged in a catastrophic Moon Shattering that killed two million souls, the DAC fills the justice vacuum where the [Council of Peace](../institutions/council_of_peace.md)'s writ cannot reach. Their creed is deceptively simple: do good. Their methods are not.

Where the COP debates jurisdiction and the [Sons of Patrah](../factions/sons_of_patrah.md) mend the wounded, the Garadar DAC rides out to meet evil at its source. The DAC's philosophical foundation, written by Zagah.eldr during his post-reforging period.

### The Five Tenets

1. **Action over contemplation** â€” Truth without action is worthless. Knowledge that does not become deed is intellectual cowardice.
2. **Universal rights** â€” All sentient beings possess inherent dignity. No species, no caste, no economic status diminishes this.
3. **Poetic justice** â€” Law serves spirit, not letter. A technically legal atrocity is still an atrocity.
4. **Individual responsibility** â€” Heroes choose, they are not born. Every being has the capacity for heroism; most simply choose comfort.
5. **No righteous waiting** â€” The greatest evil is the good person who does nothing. Inaction in the face of suffering is participation in that suffering.

## Governance â€” The DAC Model

As a Decentralized Autonomous Corporation, the Garadar DAC operates on a hybrid model that blends Ustur philosophical governance with practical military hierarchy.

- **Grand Master**: Zagah.eldr â€” moral authority, tiebreaker vote, strategic vision
- **Shield Council**: Seven elected members â€” three from Heroic Strikes, three from Balanced Deeds, one rotating civilian
- **DAO Vote**: All DAC members vote on major expeditions. Threshold: 60% approval for offensive operations, 51% for humanitarian missions
- **Contribution Shares**: Members earn governance weight through completed missions, not wealth. A first-year volunteer who completes five rescue missions outranks a wealthy donor who has never deployed

### The Recruitment Oath

Every new member speaks the words before the Shield Council:

> *"I swear to do good. I swear to act when others wait. I swear that while I live, evil must keep its eyes open."*

There is no minimum commitment. Members may leave at any time. The remarkable fact is that very few do.

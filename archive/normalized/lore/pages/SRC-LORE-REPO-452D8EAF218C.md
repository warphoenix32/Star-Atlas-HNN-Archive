## The Shadow Games

In moments of ennui, the Merchant Princes engage in **clandestine power plays** â€” espionage, sabotage, market manipulation, and intricate political maneuvering. ### The Rules (Unspoken)

1. **The games remain concealed.** No Shadow Game operation may be publicly traceable to a Merchant Prince. Deniability is absolute.
2. **Denebula's reputation is sacrosanct.** No game may damage the sector's image, economy, or infrastructure. The games are played *outside* Denebula, using pawns and proxies across the galaxy.
3. **No permanent elimination.** A Prince may be financially weakened, politically embarrassed, or strategically outmaneuvered â€” but killed? That crosses a line. (Gel's death may or may not have crossed it.)
4. **Victory is its own reward.** There is no prize for winning a Shadow Game except the knowledge that you've outplayed a peer. For beings who already own everything, this is enough.

### The Paradox

The Shadow Games make Denebula simultaneously the safest and most dangerous place in the galaxy. The planet itself is a sanctuary â€” protected, beautiful, serene. ## Economy

Denebula's economy runs on a principle borrowed from luxury markets on every civilization: **artificial scarcity creates demand.** The Princes control:

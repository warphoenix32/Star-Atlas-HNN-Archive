# Star Atlas Governance

Star Atlas governance is not a single organization or a single vote. It is a system in which POLIS holders make collective decisions, formal proposals carry those decisions, the Star Atlas Foundation provides legal and administrative capacity, and the elected Star Atlas Council supports the process and administers delegated programs. The boundaries between those roles are essential to understanding what the historical record proves.

This section follows a simple rule: **a proposal is not a vote, a vote is not implementation, and an implementation report is not independent verification**. Each page therefore separates the decision that was made from the work, payment, or outcome that may have followed.

## Begin with the institutions

- [Foundational Dossiers](../Foundational-Dossiers.md) provides the reader route across governance, treasury, economy, and the other major histories.
- [Star Atlas DAO](Star-Atlas-DAO.md) explains the POLIS electorate and its formal PVP-weighted decision authority.
- [Star Atlas Foundation](Star-Atlas-Foundation.md) describes the legal and administrative implementation body established by the captured governance framework.
- [Star Atlas Council](Star-Atlas-Council.md) traces the elected process steward, its delegated administrative work, and the limits of Council-authored operational assessments.
- [Governance Constitutional History](Governance-Constitutional-History.md) follows the system from the initial DAO framework through later institutional and funding amendments.

These institutions are related but not interchangeable. The DAO is not ATMTA. The Foundation is not the electorate. The Council does not possess an independent legislative sovereignty. The Ecosystem Fund is a policy instrument, not a fourth governing body.

## How to read the evidence

Every current-state statement carries an `as_of` date. Council tracker statements are attributed to the Council, and their independent verification status remains unknown unless a separate primary artifact is linked. Missing evidence means the archive has not yet identified the needed artifact; it does not prove that an event never happened.

Internal classifications, confidence codes, and risk labels are condensed into page metadata so the public narrative remains readable. Researchers who need the full audit trail can follow each linked Source Record, semantic registry, campaign ledger, and research gap.

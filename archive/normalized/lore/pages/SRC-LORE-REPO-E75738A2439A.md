## Overview

Vega Fall is a **grim chapter** in the annals of Sogmian history. A tale so heart-rending that to this day, uttering the names **"Vega"** or **"House Akalma"** brings visible discomfort to any Sogmian — a cultural shame comparable to how Germans view Nazism. The sector bears the name of the exposé that toppled a tyrant.

---

## The Rise of Vega

### The Luminous Gem

- In his prime, Vega was hailed as the **most luminous gem** of the Sogmian species
- Member of **House Akalma** (Crimson Red — motto: *"Embrace your emotions"*)
- Achieved a **meteoric rise** through a series of dazzling military victories
- Almost **half of the upcoming Sogmian generation** aspired to join House Akalma
- **Unanimously chosen as Sov Arei** (Supreme Leader) by peers from all noble houses

### The Consolidation

- **First act as Sov Arei**: Relocated the Sogmian capital to this sector
- Amassed unprecedented power — effectively a dictatorship
- Introduced a **heretical Du Prah interpretation**: Vows applied **exclusively to Sogmians**; other species were "uncivilized" and unworthy

---

## The Fall

### The Exposé: "Vega Fall"

- A journalist from **House Outro** infiltrated Vega's citadel
- Published the exposé **"Vega Fall"** — revealing enslavement, atrocities, and cannibalism of other species
- The revelations **united the fragmented houses** into a coalition

### The Civil War

- Devastating conflict across Sogmian space
- Culminated in **Vega's last stand** in his citadel — killed by coalition forces
- House Akalma survivors **banished** to MRZ-12 (Akalma Exile)
- This banishment led to **another great tragedy a hundred years later** — the Convergence War

---

## Current State

### House Lutavira Headquarters

- The sector is **majority Sogmian** today
- Vega's citadel has been **reformed and restored**
- It now serves as the **headquarters of House Lutavira** (Violet — *"Rise, win, conquer"*)
- Lutavira = the house of **natural-born leaders**, the most fundamentalist Du Prah followers
- Powerful symbolism: the legitimate leaders reclaiming the throne of the greatest tyrant

### Cultural Significance

- **No formal prohibition** against visiting or mentioning the sector
- The discomfort is **purely cultural** — deep collective shame, not law
- Comparable to how Germans view their Nazi past: acknowledged, studied, never celebrated
- A living reminder of what happens when the Du Prah is corrupted

### Post-War Refuge

- **~2523**: **House Outro** opens the doors of Vega Fall for **Convergence War refugees** — the sector that once symbolized Sogmian shame now becomes a beacon of compassion, sheltering the displaced from across the galaxy

### The Akalma Exile's End (~2544)

- **~2544**: The fleeing remnants of **House Akalma** encounter the **Oxir** — an enigmatic species in the HRZ
- **High Matron Odette** of the Oxir directs them to a relatively safe location in the HRZ
- Odette pleads that the Akalma survivors **live in peace, hidden from the factions** — they accept her proposal
- This marks the final known destination of the Akalma exile, and their first contact with the Oxir species

### Multi-Species Prosperity (~2586)

- **~2586**: After decades of cooperation between members of **House Outro**, **House Patrah**, and the **refugees of the Convergence War**, Vega Fall **prospers** — becoming a **beacon for the multi-species cooperation ideology**
- The sector that once symbolized Sogmian tyranny now stands as proof that different species can build something together

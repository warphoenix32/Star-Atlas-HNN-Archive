# Tollhaven (Orbital Gate-Station of Cathris)

**Sector**: Pavo Passage (MRZ-17)
**Orbits**: Cathris (capital world)
**Function**: Gate-station — where law becomes motion
**Population**: ~1 million workers
**Origin**: Crumon Dynasty relic from their brightest era

---

## Overview

Tollhaven is a city-sized orbital station circling Cathris — a mothership-scale relic of the Crumon dynasty's brightest era that still keeps the sector breathing. Nearly a million workers live inside its decks, feeding the Toll Machine with shifts, stamps, repairs, inspections, and endless waiting.

> If Cathris turns commerce into law, **Tollhaven turns law into motion**: ships that clear, ships that stall, and ships that vanish into "pending" until their captains learn what power really costs.

---

## Environment / "Feel"

Tollhaven is **layered civilization under pressure**:

**Upper rings (Crumon-era opulence)**:

- Polished halls, ceremonial docks, old prestige signage
- Architecture designed to impress travelers into obedience
- The part visitors remember — and the Conclave wants photographed

**Lower decks (service corridors)**:

- Patched bulkheads, dead sections, improvised living blocks
- Lingering consequences of past civil conflict
- The present government's struggle to keep everything moving

> It smells of lubricant, recycled air, hot metal, and overcrowding. Everything is timed, tagged, scanned, and watched — yet the most important things still happen in blind spots between shifts.

## Economy & Exports

Tollhaven's economy is **physical throughput**:

- Docking and storage at scale
- Inspection execution and reclassification handling
- Starpath route windows/slots administration
- Convoy coordination and beacon routing
- Repairs, refits, provisioning
- **Labor contracts** (the station's bloodstream)

**Its export is motion** — the ability to clear ships through the corridor.

> Proof/credential validity and registry truth are primarily anchored on Velloris, not Tollhaven.

---

## Points of Interest

### 1) The Starpath Auction Hall

Where **Starpath route usage**, time windows, and priority slots are bid for. Competition becomes ritual — public enough to look fair, controlled enough to remain political.

**Control**: Conclave calendar + operator execution; Ustur audits fairness/compliance
**Core rule**: No single operator may hold >3 active routes while the other has not secured ≥1 in the cycle.

### 2) The Gilded Concourse (Crumon Legacy Ring)

A grand public ring of ceremonial docks, legacy architecture, and "civilized" commerce — the part visitors remember, and the part the Conclave wants photographed.

**Control**: Conclave-facing administration; prestige contested by Dynasty symbolism

### 3) Queue City ("The Stacks")

A vertical sprawl of temporary living: crews waiting on holds, families displaced by delays, and contract workers living between shifts. Rumors spread faster than official notices.

**Control**: Nobody fully; VHE legitimacy is strongest here

### 4) The Inspection Spine

Gantry corridors where cargo becomes destiny: reclassified, delayed, cleared, or sealed. People fear it because it can turn one mistake into bankruptcy.

**Control**: Operators execute; Ustur standards rule; Conclave directors set priorities

### 5) Beaconworks Ridge

Routing beacons and coordination towers that feed convoy schedules into the Toll Machine. When Beaconworks stutters, the sector chokes.

**Control**: Contractors; heavily audited; quietly contested by MTC vs VHE logistics teams

### 6) VHE Workforce Commons

Hiring boards, training bays, injury support, and family relief coordination — visible proof of VHE's "people's company" posture.

**Control**: VHE civic apparatus (employment-first doctrine)

### 7) Meridian Contract Pier

A sleek dock complex where "priority" feels like a private language: better berths, smoother paperwork, faster clearances — paid for in credit and obligation.

**Control**: MTC influence through contracts/insurance gravity

### 8) The Rest House (Winter Fist Path)

A quiet shelter for injured workers and stranded crews. No violence inside. No bargaining over mercy.

**Control**: Winter Fist Path; tolerated by Conclave because it reduces unrest

## Travel Hazards

- **Hold-loop purgatory**: Your ship isn't seized, it's "pending," and pending can last forever
- **Slot sabotage**: Losing a window cascades into weeks of delay and debt
- **Audit freezes**: Ustur crackdowns can immobilize whole bays overnight
- **Deck unrest**: One unjust seizure can ignite a labor flashpoint
- **Night-shift vanishings**: People disappear from lower corridors; paperwork stays clean

---

## Story Fuel

- Tollhaven is where the sector becomes **human**: labor, overcrowding, survival, and fragile dignity
- It is also where Crumon grandeur still stands — **opulence over rot** — making the station a daily argument about what Pavo was and what it became
- **Tollhaven moves ships; Velloris moves legitimacy. Together they form the corridor's chokehold.**

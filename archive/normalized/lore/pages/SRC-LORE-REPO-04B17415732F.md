## Overview

The **Council of Peace (COP)** is the cornerstone of interstellar governance in the Galia Expanse. Born from the ashes of the Convergence War to uphold the fragile balance of power among MUD, ONI, and Ustur. Both a symbol of hope and a battleground of ideologies, where diplomacy and ambition constantly collide.

---

## Origin

- **~2523**: Created during **The Accord** (post-Convergence War settlement)
- Born alongside the **Star Atlas** blockchain and **Starpath Warp-Gates**
- Emerged from the **Treaty of Peace** signed after ONI's Reckoning campaign — all 3 faction leaders sign the Treaty, establishing open sharing of all scanned data of space, including fleet movements and installations
- Implementation led to the **Golden Era** (current canonical timeframe)

---

## Government Structure & Bureaucracy

Leadership of the three primary branches rotates every **galactic cycle (~5 Old Earth years)**. Each faction appoints their leader as representative to ensure no single faction gains undue influence.

| Branch | Current Leader | Faction | Role |
|--------|---------------|---------|------|
| **Executive** | **Volmik Vaor Scarka** | ONI (Mierese) | Ruling Secretary. Supreme executive authority. Commands the COP military. **MUD is next in rotation.** |
| **Legislative** | **Charon Gotti Jr.** | MUD (Human, female) | Political stability & cooperation. Diplomatic counterbalance to Scarka's militarism. |
| **Legal** | **Opos.eldr** | Ustur | Judicial authority. Bastion of resistance against militarism. Enacted rulings demanding cessation of MRZ hostilities. |

### Internal Tensions & Power Dynamics
*   **The Rotational Deep State:** Below the rotating leaders sits an immortal civil service of **1 billion civilian public servants**, managed by a centralized **Ministry of Personnel**. Personnel are rotated to new postings every three years to prevent local corruption.
*   **The Veto & Sabotage Balance:** While the Ruling Secretary holds formal veto power over the bureaucracy and the autonomous **Council of Ministers** (comprising all 27 departments), a wise Secretary maintains a good relationship with them. Coordinated administrative sabotage or delays from the civil service can completely paralyze an administration.
*   **The 27 Ministries:** Civilian governance is divided into 27 ministries across three tiers:
    *   **Tier 1 (Great):** Defense, Economy, Infrastructure, Intelligence, MRZ Administration, Safe Zone Administration, and the independent *Ministry of Starpath* and *Central Bank of Galia*.
    *   **Tier 2 (Standing):** Interspecies Relationships, Diplomacy, Justice, Science & Technology, Public Health, Information, Land Development & Agriculture, Environment & Ecological Affairs, Culture, Youth & Civic Education, Archives & Memory, Emergency & Crisis Management, Pensions & Social Security, Veterans & Military Welfare, and Public Servants/Personnel.
    *   **Tier 3 (Oversight/Protocol):** Morality (anti-corruption watchdog) and Rites & Etiquette.
    *   **Independent (Veto-Immune):** HRZ Affairs (SDU security), Cradle and Titanic Forces, Iris Academy, and the Star Atlas Conservatory.

## The Bureaucratic Rank System

Managed by the **Ministry of Rites and Etiquette**, the COP utilizes a dual-color uniform rank system:
*   **Uniform Base Color:** Set by the specific Ministry and Department (signaling function).
*   **Engraving Color & Body Coverage:** Set by the official's Grade, ranging from **Grade 9b** (Stone Gray, badge only) to **Grade 1a** (Pure White, full body + custom engravings).
*   **The Sector Bar:** Gated by the **Sector Examination** (required to rise past Grade 6a to enter the sector-wide Grade 5b Forest Green rank).
*   **The Galactic Bar:** Gated by the **Supreme/Cradle Examination** (required to rise past Grade 3a to enter the galaxy-wide Grade 2b Silver-White rank). Fewer than 50,000 servants hold this status.
*   **Ministers:** Sit above the numbered system with bespoke uniforms and unique heraldic engravings.

---

## The Galactic Examinations

Entry and advancement within the COP civil service are gated by three rigorous examinations:
1.  **The Entry Examination:** Open to tax-paying citizens with political rights (MRZ citizens must travel to sector capitals). High-level test on the Star Atlas Accords, COP history, AI prompting, and general sciences. Max 3 attempts. Extremely low pass rate.
2.  **The Sector Examination:** Gated by two Grade 5b+ recommendations. Involves high-pressure Holosim governance simulations, a deep audit by the Ministry of Morality, and relentless senior officer interviews. ~90% of personnel never reach this level.
3.  **The Cradle Examination (Supreme):** Gated by Grade 2b+ sponsorships and an exhaustive life audit. Candidates spend **one full simulated year in the Holosim** (dilated to one week of real-world time) working directly under their target minister. Followed by a Legislative Senate veto panel and a unique, private interview with the sentient **Star Atlas AI**.

## Military Power

### Galactic Force Index: **Tier 1** (Unmatched)

- Millions of **Pearce-class vessels**, countless advanced warships
- Billions of soldiers drawn from all three factions
- Even the **combined** fleets of MUD, ONI, and Ustur would falter against COP's scale

### Mothership Cradle (The Titan)

- **~2526**: Construction begins on the Cradle — the most powerful vessel ever built.
- **~2617**: Construction of the Cradle is **completed** — the most powerful vessel in galactic history is operational.
- Equipped with unmatched technological advancements.
- Capable of overwhelming even the most formidable opponents.
- **Recently deployed**: Against Pergamos and Frenir in the MRZ — sent shockwaves across the galaxy.

---

## Economic Power

### Galactic Wealth Index: **Tier 1** (Wealthiest Institution)

- **Starpath Warp Gates** are operated by the **Star Atlas** (sentient blockchain) independently, but Star Atlas works in harmony with COP and submits to its legal jurisdiction.
- Regulatory authority over galactic trade ensures stability.
- Funds large-scale reconstruction, military, and bureaucratic apparatus through taxation and trade regulation.

---

## Political Power

### Galactic Political Index: **Tier 1** (Ultimate Authority)

- Primary governing body in Galia Expanse.
- Rulings dictate trade flow, territorial disputes, and foundational laws (**Star Atlas Accords**).
- Tri-faction leadership ensures no single faction dominates.
- Collective authority supersedes even the most powerful individual factions.

| Institution | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| **Council of Peace** | Galactic governance and peacekeeping |
| **Star Atlas** | Economic infrastructure (sentient blockchain) |
| **Starpath Warp-Gates** | Physical infrastructure connecting sectors across the void. **~2526**: Construction begins. **~2596**: Construction ends — ushering in **prosperity across the Safe Zone** |

### ECOS Membership

- **~2527**: The **ECOS** (independent environmental/ecological faction) attempts to gain **major faction status** within the COP. The **MUD lobby** successfully blocks this, relegating ECOS to a **member without voting rights**.

### The Current Age

- **~2620**: The **Current Age** of Star Atlas begins — the present canonical timeframe in which the galaxy's political tensions, faction rivalries, and unresolved mysteries drive the narrative forward.
- **~2621**: **Vaor Scarka** becomes **Ruling Secretary** of the Council of Peace (ONI's turn in rotation)
- **~2623**: The **Council of Peace intervenes in the Medium Zone** — COP forces attack **Frenir**, **Pergamos**, and the **Jorvik Fleet** in a decisive military campaign
- **~2623**: The **Events of Never Alone** begin — a major canonical narrative arc
- **~2623**: Under pressure from MRZ factions (led by **Redam**) and internal faction leadership, the COP **accepts a truce** in the Medium Zone, limiting its military activities

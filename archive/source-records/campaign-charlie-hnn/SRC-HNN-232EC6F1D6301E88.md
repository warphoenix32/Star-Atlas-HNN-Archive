Which Star Atlas Faction has the Upper Hand? (Part II)
Posted by Langwenya | Aug 17, 2022 | Politics
“…set in the year 2620. The universe has been colonized, and balkanized. Three factions split control, while somewhere in the deepest, most dangerous sector of space, something extremely valuable lurks. These 3 Star Atlas factions are: MUD , ONI and USTUR…”
Precept
Continuing from Part I where I discussed the different characteristics of the three Star Atlas factions (MUD, ONI, and USTUR) and the distinct advantages each faction has over the others. Every advantage and disadvantage places each faction in a unique position within the metaverse. In an unforgiving environment where each of the factions are constantly battling for superiority in resource extraction, manufacturing, firepower, and advancements in technological capabilities, etc.
Part II of this series will look into whether a dominating faction has already been established. It will consider the different metrics by which dominance or superiority in the Star Atlas metaverse can be measured. But as the game has not fully launched, or at least to a point where things like faction traits can play a role, for now the focus will be on each faction’s current social engagement, population, resources, and economic metrics.
Social Engagement
Social engagement may not be characterised as an in-game mechanism or even considered an important metric for measuring a faction’s dominance/superiority over the others. But for a game as immersive as Star Atlas, social interaction is a powerful metric for factions to consider. Star Atlas is a metaverse game whose implications extend beyond the digital realm. It is a game played by real people across the world, with personal connections and in-game wealth directly translating to the real world. Social Media then also is undoubtedly the best tool with which an individual or group of individuals build connections in the digital space. Considering that people are social beings; social media is a very effective tool in recruiting new members to your chosen faction.
Dominance over social engagements on social media means that a faction has a louder voice over the others, which it can utilize to attract attention and demonstrate to that audience it’s superior qualities. Essentially helping the faction to sign up new members to join the faction which then further amplifies its voice. The beauty of Star Atlas is that it is multifaceted, meaning it can be strategically approached from different angles depending on one’s goals in the game. This is something that’s apparent with how different guilds approach member recruitment; some strongly emphasize social engagement and building a strong community, while others focus on spaceship acquisitions to ensure they have the biggest fleet in the game.
Either way you look at it, the data shows that there’s strenghth in numbers. The more people you recruit, the more powerful your faction becomes. And what better way to do that than through social media?
A couple of months back Star Atlas’ official Twitter account posted a series of tweets targeted to each of the factions. Have a look!
These images show that the MUD faction has (or at least had) a superior social presence compared to the other two factions on Twitter. In other words, this means MUD had a social media voice that’s much louder than the others and this would’ve made it easier for them to get their message across and recruit new members.
Although the above tweets were posted 7 months ago, they help to showcase the importance and impact that a strong social media representantion can have on faction growth. Which takes us to the next dominance metric.
Population
This is how the Star Atlas’ population 0f almost a hundred thousand (99 143) is currently distributed across the three factions. As can be seen on the Star Atlas leaderboard page.
ONI – 36 244 (1st)
MUD – 32 205 (– 11.09%)
USTUR – 30 694 (-15.31)
Much like having a strong social presence, having a large population has many benefits. In most cases, the benefits of having a large population correllate closely with dominance on the other metrics. For example, having a larger population means you can easily dominate the ‘social engagement’ metric. Also, it makes it easier to dominate the economy because the more people you have generally means more money invested and more assets acquired.
On the flipside, when SCREAM launches, having a smaller population will have its own benefits. For instance, members of a smaller faction (population) will earn more compared to members of larger factions. This is due to the fact that all factions are distributed an equal amount of resources which are shared across all members. Basically, it means members of smaller factions will earn bigger slices of the pie. With this mechanism in place it gives new people joining the game an incentive to join factions with a smaller population, which should help balance out the populations of all faction. At least that’s how it should work in theory.
That’s also where opportunity comes in play for the factions: Can the incentives/rewards of joining a larger faction outweigh those of joining a smaller one? It seems it’s in each faction’s best interest to gain a larger population despite the fact that resources will have to be shared with many more people. Also, I mentioned that people are social beings, which means they are much more likely to go where the crowd is, and most people also feel safer and more secure in larger groups. With that said, resources are extremely valuable in the Star Atlas metaverse and should carry a huge incentive weight to the discerning player.
Resources
This is where last place jumps into first place! As stated above, the faction with the least members will have bigger shares of the resources distributions. In this case, members of the USTUR faction should have 15% more resource share than ONI who have the largest population, and 4% more than MUD with the second largest population.
Resources are what drive the economy and ambitions of players within Star Atlas because they fuel the very same ships that go out and look for these resources on other planets, while at the same time helping defend the resource at homebase. Resources that have been extracted or mined will also need to be manufactured or crafted into desired products.
That last past is vital because when SCREAM launches later this year, Star Atlas will no longer provide R4 resources (ammunition, food, fuel, and toolkits) in the marketplace. Instead, factions will start mining their own resources and manufacturing for products that they need. In other words, with its advantage in the amount of resources it’ll have, USTUR has the opportunity to craft for itself a competitive and powerful economy in the metaverse. Largely due to the fact that its smaller population won’t consume as much resources, allowing for the sale of its reserve/spare resources to other factions who might be in desperate need of them.
With everything we’ve considered thus far, resources is where the game will soon be centered once Star Atlas halts its supplies to the market in a couple of weeks. As it is, factions and individual players are scrambling to load up on as much resources as they can while they’re still available on the market. But focusing on just the purchase of resources means something else, like the purchase of ships or directing funds toward member recruitment efforts, is being neglected. And this can tip the scale of power/dominance.
See the analogy I make to the game of rock, paper, scissors in Part I.
Economy
A faction’s economy is a measure of its current position in the hierarchal scheme of Star Atlas. But, again, as you would’ve gauged, a factions current position does not guarantee it’ll stay there in the future as there are already many opposing forces.
Here’s what the faction leaderboard looks like at present. But note that the current leaderboard doesn’t take accurate accounting of some assets including claims, structures, and airdrops etc.
Total asset value collectively takes into account all the ships, collectibles, badges and licences, structures, and currency (ATLAS & POLIS) owned by individual members of a faction. As seen in the image the MUD faction has 21.8% and 29% more total asset value than the ONI and USTUR factions, respectively. Also, not shown in this image, is that 2 of the top 3 richest accounts on Star Atlas belong to members of the MUD faction, with the 4th richest account also being of MUD affiliation.
Here are the ownership stats of ships and currencies for all the factions.
[CREDIT: The Club’s Factions Asset Charts]
Despite its big population advantage over the MUD faction, also seen with its greater number of assets holders, ONI still possesses a lower asset value. Which, at this point, suggests the obvious. And that is; the MUD faction has more whales in its member ranks, whereas ONI has more minnows. It’s also not surprising that USTUR has both the lowest population and the least wealth.
Who has the Upper Hand?
ONI
ONI boasts the largest population in the Star Atlas metaverse which should give it many advantages if that member resource is utilized to its utmost potential. With that many members, ONI needs to at least be dominating the social engagement metric – utilizing their strength in numbers to get all of their messages across and the loudest. Enabling them to recruit more members and thus acquiring more wealth.
But as ONI dominates the population metric, it doesn’t match up in wealth (total asset value).
MUD
MUD picks up right where ONI lacks, and that’s in wealth (total asset value). Despite having a much lower population, the MUD faction has managed to attract the biggest whales in the Star Atlas metaverse. This single fact has helped the faction to acquire and control by far the most wealth in the game. But this also raises some questions as to whether or not this faction’s wealth is too centralized, especially when looking at ONI’s wealth distribution.
Depending on how the whales choose to utilize their wealth, this could of great concern to the MUD faction. And where there’s a weakness, someone else sees an opportunity.
USTUR
USTUR has the least wealth and the smallest population. However, they have arguably the most valuable thing in the whole of the Star Atlas metaverse, and that’s resources. Because of it’s smaller population, USTUR members will receive the largest share of the resources that practically fuel the entire economy of Star Atlas. Now, having mentioned how the wealthiest faction (MUD) has the richest whales in the metaverse; assets such as ships, infrastructure, and production/manufacturing buildings (starbases) will all require the resources that USTUR has an abundance of in order to function.
If USTUR utilizes it’s great advantage of abundant resources, then it could turn the tide in its favour. Just the mere thought that one faction yields additional resources over all the others should quickly attract more members, and potentially even bigger whales. This would probably lead to USTUR taking over the current position of MUD in the coming months.
Who has the upper hand?
That’s a question I’ll leave up to you to decide.

# The Galia Expanse

The Galia Expanse is the principal setting of Star Atlas: a vast inhabited region organized through star systems, worlds, political territories, trade routes, dangerous frontiers, and the gravitational memory of earlier wars. Its history is shared by three major factions—Manus Ultima Divina, the ONI Consortium, and the Ustur—but the Expanse is not reducible to those powers. It also contains independent peoples, minor states, corporations, religious orders, criminal networks, and places whose loyalties do not fit neatly inside the major-faction map.

This page describes the setting as represented in the Archive's captured lore repository. It is a guide to the world, not a claim that every coordinate, resource table, officeholder, or territorial boundary is permanently current.

## A galaxy organized by risk and reach

Star Atlas lore commonly distinguishes safe, medium-risk, and high-risk space. The categories carry more than a gameplay meaning. They help describe the degree of factional control, the reach of institutions, the security of travel and commerce, and the distance from the settled political order.

The captured *Galia Expanse Atlas* organizes its data as **region → star system → planet → resources**. At the captured commit it reports 69 regions and 945 star systems. Those counts belong to that source snapshot. They are useful for studying the structure of the upstream dataset, but later world-building, game development, or revised exports may change them.

## Three major powers

[Manus Ultima Divina](Manus-Ultima-Divina.md), usually called MUD, is the human major faction. Its lore connects religious destiny, dynastic government, commercial power, and humanity's exodus from Earth.

The [ONI Consortium](ONI-Consortium.md) is a multi-species alliance associated with Sogmian, Punaab, Photoli, and Mierese peoples. Its founding narrative grows out of exile, wartime necessity, and the choice to make cooperation an institution.

The [Ustur](Ustur.md) are both a species and a factional civilization. Their lore begins with awakening on Ioki and develops through the Path of Enlightenment, advanced industry, and an approach to identity in which memory, continuity, and the cultivated core have unusual importance.

The faction labels describe political civilizations, not every individual member of a species. They also should not be used to erase unaffiliated communities or internal institutions.

## A political order born from catastrophe

The [Convergence War](Convergence-War.md) is the principal turning point in the captured inter-faction history. The source narrative connects conflict around Iris, the exploitation of the Tufa, the near-destruction and exile of the Sogmian, the formation of ONI, and a final settlement among the major powers.

The [Council of Peace](Council-of-Peace.md) emerges from that settlement as the shared institution through which MUD, ONI, and Ustur attempt to manage galactic order. The Council does not make the factions disappear. It overlays them with a rotating structure whose authority, ministries, and officeholders are described in the source snapshot.

## Peoples, places, and scales of history

The [Peoples of Galia](Peoples-of-Galia.md) include humans, Ustur, the four peoples most closely associated with ONI, and other communities such as the Tufa, Crumon, and Oxir. Their histories range from established interstellar civilizations to peoples documented only in short reference pages.

That unevenness matters. A long source page may offer theology, political institutions, named leaders, and centuries of chronology. A shorter page may establish only a name, home region, or a few traits. The Archive preserves both without filling the thinner record with inference.

The same distinction applies to geography. A region can be a canonical named place while its current controlling faction, game resources, or system count remains time-bounded or unresolved.

## How to use this setting guide

For names, entity classes, and aliases, consult the [Canon Registry](Canon-Registry.md). For the real-world order in which lore was published, use the [Lore Publication Timeline](../timeline/Lore-Timeline.md). For source-level research, follow the evidence paths in each page.

The most important boundary is between **in-universe chronology** and **publication chronology**. The Convergence War may be dated inside the fiction, while the pages describing it were written and revised at real-world dates that the Archive has not fully recovered. Neither timeline should be used as a substitute for the other.

## Current interpretation

As of this review, the Galia Expanse is best understood as a layered setting rather than a static map: three major factions operate within a broader field of peoples and institutions, the Convergence War explains much of the current political order, and the Council of Peace represents an attempted settlement rather than the end of rivalry.

That synthesis is well supported by the captured lore taxonomy and core faction pages. Exact map counts, territorial control, present officeholders, and some detailed chronology remain snapshot claims pending comparison with later official lore.

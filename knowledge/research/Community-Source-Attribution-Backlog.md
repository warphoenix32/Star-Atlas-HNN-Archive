# Community Source Attribution Backlog

This file consolidates unresolved attribution and source-lineage questions identified during the first audited community-source campaigns.

## Aephia

- Resolve articles with incomplete or ambiguous author metadata.
- Reconcile older operational guides and roadmap claims against later releases.
- Identify linked primary-source broadcasts or official posts where the article references them without durable identifiers.

## Intergalactic Herald

- Identify the original creator and publication for records with incomplete source lineage.
- Distinguish original reporting from curated or repackaged work where the surviving page is ambiguous.
- Recover broken or deleted primary-source links referenced by Herald articles.
- Standardize free-text original-creator names to canonical entity IDs.

## Hologram News Network and related identities

- Recover missing bylines without inferring authorship from ownership.
- Reconstruct the relationship and active periods of `hologramnews.com`, `thehologram.io`, The Hologram on Medium, and Krigs's personal Medium publications.
- Identify missing or deleted HNN pages through public archives.
- Separate contemporaneous HNN records from later first-party recollections.

## Cross-source questions

- Establish canonical IDs for recurring publications, shows, creators, and host organizations.
- Preserve the distinction among publisher, host, broadcaster, curator, interviewer, and original creator.
- Identify duplicate coverage of the same event without collapsing distinct publications.
- Determine when a secondary source contains substantial original reporting that merits dual classification.

## Resolution rule

An attribution question is closed only when supported by explicit page metadata, a surviving linked source, a public statement by the creator or publisher, or multiple corroborating records. Unknown attribution should remain visible.

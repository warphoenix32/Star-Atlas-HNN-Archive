## The Geopolitical Landscape (100 Years Post-War)

### The Great Retreat & The Golden Age

After the **Treaty of Peace**, the major factions (MUD, ONI, Ustur) retreated to their home systems (Safe Zones) to rebuild.

- **Inside**: A "Golden Age". Vaults full of ATLAS, societies rebuilt, stability enforced by the Council of Peace (COP).
- **The Pressure**: The Safe Zones are now bursting at the seams. Borders are "simply not enough anymore".

### 🟢 Universal Free Movement (Canon Rule)

**The Treaty of Peace guarantees free circulation for ALL species across ALL of Galia.** Every sector — Safe Zone, MRZ, or HRZ — has members of every species present. "Influence" means the faction that founded/controls the sector politically and economically, NOT that only their species live there.

### The Power Vacuum (The Outer Worlds)

The retreat left the **Medium** and **High Risk Zones** abandoned.

- **The Betrayal**: Colonies were explored, conquered, and then left to fend for themselves when the armies went home.
- **The Resentment**: The COP's "peace" is viewed as a system that privileges the inner factions while the outer worlds suffer.
- **The Result**: Chaos, warlords, and the rise of **Minor Factions** (Jorvik, ECOS) filling the void.

## The Three Zones

### 1. Safe Zone ( The Home Systems)

- **Status**: **Security / Wealth / Stagnation**
- **Sectors**:
  - **Exodus** (MUD / Human)
  - **Segal** (Sogmian / ONI 4)
  - **Akenat** (Punaab / ONI 4)
  - **Neuno** (Mierese / ONI 4)
  - **Usturvana** (Ustur)
- **Atmosphere**: High-tech, safe, patrolled by COP. "Inebriated by divine providence" (MUD) or "Shielded by Vows" (Sogmian).
- **Conflict**: Political maneuvering, espionage, internal faction riots (e.g., MUD's church vs military).

### 2. Medium Risk Zone (MRZ) - "The Third World"

- **Status**: **Inequality / Opportunity / Instability**
- **Atmosphere**: Resembles third-world inequality.
  - **The Elite**: Wealthy regional powers trading rare resources with the Safe Zone.
  - **The Masses**: Impoverished, serving the elite or surviving in scrap-towns.
- **Dynamics**:
  - **Warlords**: Regional powers trying to seize control.
  - **Raids**: Constant attacks from the HRZ.
  - **Supply Lines**: Isolated from main faction routes.
  - **Factions**: ECOS (Ideological rebels), Jorvik (Scavengers/Pirates).

### 3. High Risk Zone (HRZ) - "The Graveyard"

- **Status**: **Ruins / Tufa / Cataclysm**
- **Atmosphere**: Civilizations here collapsed. Only ruins and danger remain.
- **Threats**:
  - **Tufa Attacks**: Constant predation.
  - **Environment**: Deep space anomalies, creatures.
  - **The Cataclysm**: The sealed territory of Iris.
- **Population**: Only the desperate, the insane, or the extremely powerful venture here.
- **~2499**: Tales of the riches found in the inner places of the galaxy start to spread — **daring captains begin exploring** the area known as the High-risk zone
- **~2507**: Factions start to create the **first HRZ outposts** — formalizing the exploration into permanent presence

---

## The Council of Peace (COP) Position

- **Intent**: Genuine concern for the outer worlds (mostly). Attempts to make amends.
- **Reality**: constrained. Lacks resources/political capital to intervene effectively in the MRZ/HRZ without angering the major factions.
- **Perception**: Seen as a puppet of the Safe Zone elites by those living in the MRZ.

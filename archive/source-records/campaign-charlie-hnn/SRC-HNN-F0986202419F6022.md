How to find your Star Atlas Ship NFT
Posted by FowlAC | Aug 15, 2022 | Economics, Featured
Nice! You bought a Pearce X6. It’s sitting there in your Phantom wallet and looks great. But what exactly did you buy and what do you really own? What follows is a quick tutorial for finding your Star Atlas NFT.
Step 1: Go to https://galaxy.staratlas.com/nfts
Galaxy is where all Star Atlas NFT content is stored and yes, it’s nearly impossible to read without a JSON viewer. The JSON viewer extension will convert the page into a more readable format. I recommend downloading and activating one from the Chrome extension store. Note: You may need to restart Chrome to get it to work.
Galaxy contains the same content as the Star Atlas marketplace (just not as pretty). If the devs make a change in Galaxy, it will change the information in the Star Atlas marketplace. Keep in mind that Galaxy is not a static site. Devs are frequently adding new content (e.g., ships) and even modifying existing content.
Step 2: Use the CTRL F shortcut to find a ship that is in your fleet.
Let’s use the Pearce X6 as an example so type it into the find box. Once you’ve jumped down to the X6 section you will see the description and specs of the ship.
If you click on any of the storage.googleapis.com links you will also see the images associated with your Pearce X6. A couple of things to keep in mind: Galaxy content is publicly available and stored in the Google Cloud. If you own a Pearce X6 NFT you don’t own any of this content. The IP is owned by Star Atlas.
Step 3: Find the mint address.
If you continue to scroll down you will eventually see the “mint” address. If you copy and paste that address into Solscan you will see that 8000 Pearce X6s were minted. This is proof of mint and that they exist on the blockchain. In Solscan you can also see recent transactions, other holders and some metadata. If you click the little yellow bird icon in the “Social Channels” you can get additional data and charts.
If you click the “Metadata” tab, you will see that it is currently set to “isMutable.” This effectively means that the Pearce X6 is not an NFT (non-fungible token) but rather an SFT (semi fungible token). Simply stated, an SFT means that the digital asset properties can be changed at will. There are many good reasons for allowing this but if you were thinking your ship can never be changed, that is not the case.
Step 4: Locate your ship purchase transaction.
This is much harder to find. First, you will need the address of the Solana wallet where you purchased the ship. If you simply add that address into Solscan you will have to look through each transaction. If you have an active wallet this could take forever, especially if you’ve been using that wallet to resource your ship in SCORE.
A better option is to use stake.tax. Select the Sol tab and then enter in your wallet address. It will create a CSV file containing a history of all your transactions. Again, depending on how active your wallet address is, it may take a while for the CSV file to be created. Once the file is created scroll through the rows until you see the thumbnail image of the ship you’re looking for. It’ll look like this:
Click the transaction ID (TXID) and it will show you your purchase on the Solana blockchain. Be aware that if your ship is enlisted in SCORE there will be second thumbnail with the FLEET prefix. This is not your ship. Rather verification that your ship is enlisted.
So what does this transaction mean? Technically, it represents an IOU from Star Atlas for a specific ship token. In fact, this transaction on the blockchain is the only thing that is unique to you and proves you own anything in Star Atlas.

Final Thoughts
Personally, going through this process was important and helped me to understand NFTs, SFTs and how they are represented on the blockchain. It also was helped me to understand my rights as an NFT/SFT owner and some of the inherent risks associated with participating in blockchain gaming.
Star Atlas has done its job by providing the information and being available to answer questions. However, the final step still must be taken by you. Take the time to educate yourself (and others).

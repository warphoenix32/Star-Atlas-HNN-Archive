A Moment That Lasted A Lifetime
Posted by Hardass | Jun 2, 2022 | Community
It was another Saturday that I was away from my family. As I jump to the star system that I cannot disclose due to security concerns, I notice the dust and rubbish that has collected in the cockpit of my Fimbul Packlite. “I bought it for trade, not for the looks” I thought to myself and promised I would clean it really well before I told my family of the trade opportunities that will be open to me with the increased jump rangefrom working on engineering my warp drive. I pointed to the engineer’s base on a secret planet located several hours at warp speed away from the main star and engaged super cruise assist.
The button was sticky from the coffee I spilled, so I promised to myself to clean the cockpit before I docked to meet the notorious engineer. I didn’t want her to have a bad first impression of me.
As I was getting a rag to clean the keyboard, I received a red warning message of WARP DRIVE INTERDICTION.
“What’s going on? Damn it, I thought everything was working properly just a minute ago!” Then I saw the red hollow triangle behind me on the minimap. I couldn’t see the name of the commander that interdicted me. I checked the comms and there was no message from anyone. Before I could realize, the interdiction was complete and a heavily armed fighter ship was behind me shooting plasma at my thrusters.
I panicked.
I wanted to write to him to stop, but my thruster drives were off already and my hull was down to 50%. A moment later, my hull was down to 20% and I knew it was over for me. I was never a religious man, but at that moment I closed my eyes trying to remember the prayers that my mother taught me.
Suddenly there she was in front of me, stroking my hair and saying “my dear boy, wherever this life takes you you can always come back home”. I saw my sisters playing with their dolls. Then my father appeared in front of me telling me to be kind and never act in anger.
I saw my flight instructor laughing as she looked at my confused face while trying to remember the positioning of the controls in the cockpit of my training vessel. Then I heard a laugh of an angel: I saw Anya in her wedding dress smile as she looked at me and said “Now you are mine and I am yours”. I saw my newborn daughter make funny faces and giggle as I lifted her up. I remembered hugging her for the last time.
I forgave everyone who has ever done me harm and accepted my tragic fate with a feeling of regret for not spending all my time with my family. Then I heard my communicator beep and I opened my eyes. My ship was badly damaged, the hull was at 7% and the damage was critical with most of the systems failing.
I brought up the comms panel and saw a message from a commander: “You are safe but you have to do as I tell you NOW! Pull up your ship’s menu then reboot your systems. You will have your systems back up”
As I read that, I checked what was going on around me on the radar. My savior gently floated in front of my cockpit in a matte charcoal gray Pearce X6 which I could barely distinguish from the dark space around him. I used the radar to target objects around me. 7 km away I saw 2 more commanders attacking the terrorist who was about to end my life for his sadistic pleasure. I saw as the two commanders that were engaging the terrorist spin around in unison while each was taking hits from the terrorist’s plasmas.
With a sense of urgency and my ship literally falling apart, I did as the Pearce X6 pilot instructed. I remembered this from my flight school training but I had never rebooted the systems before. As I confirmed the system reboot of the ship, the pilot that flew towards me to help me get my systems back up sent another message: “Jump to Cenniceninno system, there is a repair station there, do it now.”
As I picked out the system from the navigation pane and charged my Warp Drive, I saw my saviour boost towards the terrorist and unload a barrage of superheated rounds into his hull. Just before the warp drive engaged, I wanted to thank them. I wanted to honor them by calling my son in the name of one of them, but all I could manage before the swoosh sound of my ship jumping into a wormhole filled my cockpit, was to stand up and salute them as tears were going down my cheeks…

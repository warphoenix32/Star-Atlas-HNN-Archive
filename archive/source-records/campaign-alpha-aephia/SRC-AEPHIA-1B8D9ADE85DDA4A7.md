Welcome to our 135th newsletter on Star Atlas! This weekly newsletter, published by Aephia Industries, focuses entirely on the development of this ambitious game. Here, we try to aggregate all the newsworthy tidbits revealed [mainly by the team] throughout the past week.
Last week was a big week for SAGE Labs: Starbased, as two long-awaited changes were finally rolled out! On top of that, less expected, the team rolled out their new Meta Crafting Station, enabling players to craft items from outside of the official Star Atlas universe. But that is not all; the team also announced the date for the next Town Hall, which is a little further away than we thought: Wednesday, July 3rd.
But let’s not get ahead of ourselves! Let’s dive in!

Starbased Update
The team rolled out several updates to Starbased on June 20th, and below, we’ll go over them one by one!
SDU Component Cost Update
Last week finally saw the rollout of the much-anticipated SDU Component Cost update. From now on, upgrading Starbases to the next Tier will be much cheaper (SDU-wise). We published the new numbers in our previous issue, and you can, of course, also find these updated in our Starbased Guide.
On the topic of scanning, one additional balancing change the team made was lowering the Scan Sector Cooldown from 10 minutes to 9 minutes.
Automatic LP Contribution
To quote the team: “In an effort to make ATLAS earnings easier to understand and make the game more resilient to manipulation, which negatively impacts certain player demographics, LP will now automatically be deposited towards the epoch’s LP pool upon receiving it.” Players will no longer receive LP to deposit at a time of their choosing. This is a deviation from the original plans, where LP would still find its way to players, even though it would also be registered for the daily ATLAS payout. But there would have been two issues with this, which the team simply solved by not handing out LP at all:
LP can not be spent – there is no LP store yet, so LP would just accumulate without having an actual use case
it would introduce a double-spend problem over the next two weeks
LP Deposit Deprecation
On this last note, the team will allow everyone to deposit their saved-up Loyalty Points to acquire part of the daily ATLAS emissions over a two-week period that started June 20th (when these changes were released). After that, the LP deposit feature will be deactivated.

Starbased – The new Meta Crafting Station
Meta Crafting Station
The team added a brand-new Meta Crafting Station to SAGE Labs! This allows players to craft items from outside the Star Atlas ecosystem. Though we were expecting this to be added at some point, its release just this past week came unannounced and unexpected.
The very first recipe added is one for a limited edition poster created by the SuperphoenixDAO, dubbed The Edge of Paradise.
Honeyland seems to be next in line to get a Meta Crafting recipe added, with an event scheduled for later today (21:00 UTC) on June 24th. Both Corey (CEO of Hexagon Studios) and Michael (CEO of ATMTA) will be revealing more information about this collaboration. Originally, this collaboration was supposed to go live at the beginning of the year, but in the end, that did not come to fruition due to planning issues on ATMTA’s side.

Starbased – Feature Freeze
With this latest update, the team announced a temporary pausing of the regular updates to Starbased. This means that the team won’t make any mechanical (gameplay) changes going forward. Numbers are a different story, so Chris would still be able to rebalance some numbers if he wanted to.
The reason for this is that the team wants to focus on getting Fleet Command out first. In order to do that, they need to reach feature parity between the two different SAGE products. Complicating this is the reality that it is harder to implement UI changes into the 3D experience of Fleet Command than it is to do so in the SAGE Labs interface.
In other words, any change they make to Starbased requires them to put even more time into Fleet Command. If they would keep doing that, they will never be able to get to the point where the two products offer the exact same feature set.
Note that this freeze will not affect the rollout of the Crew packs and Crew-based functionality in Starbased. Crew will still come online before Fleet Command, as planned.

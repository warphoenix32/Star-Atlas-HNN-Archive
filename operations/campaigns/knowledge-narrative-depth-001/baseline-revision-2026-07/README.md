# Knowledge Baseline Revision 2026-07

This follow-on campaign deliberately reviews every Markdown page under `knowledge/` against the repository's human-first authoring, evidence, lifecycle, taxonomy, and public-presentation standards.

The inventory is diagnostic. It does not declare existing claims false and does not promote semantic candidates. Each later domain wave must re-open the underlying Source Records and evidence packets before changing material claims.

## Revision principles

- Preserve stable paths whenever possible.
- Update existing pages before creating replacements.
- Write connected, comprehensive historical or institutional narratives for human readers.
- Keep machine taxonomy in front matter or one consolidated reference section.
- Preserve proposal, vote, passage, authorization, payment, implementation, testing, release, completion, and independent verification as distinct states.
- Date current-state claims and expose limitations and research gaps.
- Use ethical SEO metadata and descriptive headings without keyword stuffing.
- Treat transcript evidence according to claim-specific speaker dependency.
- Exclude unrelated politics, unrelated games, and off-topic attacks from Discord-derived knowledge.
- Require individual human adjudication for R3/R4 and reputationally adverse interpretations.

## Planned waves

1. Governance and economy.
2. Gameplay, technology, events, and timelines.
3. Organizations, guilds, people, media, and source registries.
4. Lore, research, root navigation, indexes, and controversies.

Each wave receives its own branch and draft PR. A wave may merge only after its evidence, references, narrative quality, metadata, internal links, and repository-scope checks pass.

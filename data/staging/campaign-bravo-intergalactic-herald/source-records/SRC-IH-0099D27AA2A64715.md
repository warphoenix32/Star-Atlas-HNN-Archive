Weekly Star Atlas Newsletter #213 from Aephia – https://aephia.com/star-atlas/weekly-star-atlas-newsletter-213/
The most impactful announcement this week is the restructuring of ATMTA’s executive leadership, as Santi, the VP of Community and Ecosystem, steps down from his full-time role to pursue a new entrepreneurial venture, though he will remain involved as an adviser. Simultaneously, the development team shared critical insights into upcoming game modules. Holosim Chapter 2 is anticipated for release in late January or early February, featuring fully voiced NPC dialogue for lore-wrapped Faction quests and implementing initial automation features derived from collaboration with the SLY Automation Tool authors. For SAGE C4, the development path has involved significant foundational work, including the adoption of the custom *Star Frame* SDK and a shift to the *SolidJS* framework for enhanced performance, leading to the confirmation that the PTR launch will include all four core features: Council Rank, Combat, Crafting Habs, and Claim Stakes. Furthermore, the UE5 client received two minor patches correcting critical bugs in Racing and Siege modes (including a notable fix for AMD GPU rendering issues), and the creative team announced a forthcoming 12-part lore book series titled *The Convergence War*.
The Atlas Brew #188 from Star Atlas TV – https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=stqqz8li9_Y
The latest Star Atlas “Brew” provided significant updates on the upcoming Zinc L1 network, a custom fork of the Solana Virtual Machine designed to deliver a high-performance, low-cost environment ideal for gaming. Developers confirmed the Zinc Testnet has been running successfully for roughly 90 days, with the primary remaining hurdle being the finalization and auditing of the SVM-to-SVM bridging solution and securing launch partners for DeFi piping. The shift to Zinc is crucial for the upcoming *HoloSim* Chapter 2, which introduces massive gameplay changes, including significantly slower combat (battles may last up to 10 minutes) allowing for strategic reinforcement and counter-attacks, alongside new PvE elements. The team emphasized that while the core SVM rental fee structure remains, the effective cost will be pennies, making free-to-play sustainable. Builders are being actively sought to leverage the Zinc Profile and Multi-Pass reward system, ensuring a high-quality ecosystem focused on attracting gaming talent and integrated DeFi services that avoid the typical high costs and congestion of public L1s.
Star Atlas Economic Forum January 2026 from Star Atlas TV – https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jGj_81l3-7g
The January Star Atlas Economic Forum delivered highly bullish results, focusing primarily on the impact of the Open Market Operations (OMO) program and the early demonstration of C4. An econometric Difference-in-Differences (DiD) analysis of the first OMO batch confirmed the program’s success, resulting in a positive relative price action of **$20.53** for OMO-listed assets compared to non-OMO assets. Critically, the program generated a **6,259% increase in primary sales revenue** for Automata (rising from $130 pre-OMO to $8,267 post-OMO). Building on this success, the team announced a new batch of OMO listings expected on January 26, 2024, featuring Claim Stakes (Tier 5 listed at **$4,202.50**) and various Armstrong and Fimbul assets, all offered at new market-aligned prices. Furthermore, the team shared massive excitement following the first demo of C4, confirming the game’s economic loop will focus heavily on **combat and conquest** as the primary means of earning, completely eliminating the previous liquidity provisioning (LP) mechanism. Community members can also expect an immediate update to the current redemption recipes, which are currently priced too high.

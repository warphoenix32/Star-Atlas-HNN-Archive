## Overview

**Old Grove** stands as a stark testament to MUD's cautionary tales against ECOS. Once ECOS's envisioned utopian paradise — now a **verdant nightmare** of uncontrolled mutation.

## Key Lore

### ECOS's Failed Eden

- **~2401**: ECOS dissidence happens; dissident group leaves MUD and retreats towards Old Grove Sector
- **~2435**: After failing to reach an agreement, ECOS **destroys the civilization** occupying Old Grove and begins experiments to "rebalance natural life"
- **Public Knowledge**: This genocide is **known across the galaxy**. ECOS spins it as "a necessary step where the technology accidentally spiraled out of control." MUD uses it as propaganda.

### What Went Wrong

- **~2439**: ECOS terraforming goes wrong — Old Grove's central planet ecosystem **spirals out of control**; enormous verdant monsters start to spawn from forest-engulfed ruins
- **~2439**: ECOS gets overwhelmed and **flees to Hanging Gardens** (MRZ-16)
- Whether from **nascent flaws** in ECOS's then-new terraforming technology or **unexpected reactions** with residual industrial contaminants — the outcome was catastrophic

### Current State

- **Abandoned by ECOS**. They retreated to safer domains: **Hanging Gardens** (MRZ-16, ECOS MRZ stronghold) and their true base: **Earth Prime** in the HRZ.
- **~2465**: Small mining colonies begin forming on outer ring planets, where the verdant nightmare is still under control
- Relentless mutated wilderness threatens to consume all traces of intervention
- Only a **handful of fortified outposts** endure — on borrowed time
- **Mutations contained** within the sector — for now
- **~2552**: A group of **MUD entrepreneurs** attempts to create a settlement on the **main planet**, but after months of activity they are **completely overwhelmed** by the mutated flora and fauna — further cementing Old Grove's reputation as an impossible frontier

# History of Star Atlas Guilds and DACs

Guilds and Decentralized Autonomous Corporations have organized much of the player side of Star Atlas. They have published guides, built economic tools, trained new players, run events, competed, produced media, joined governance debates, and carried rivalries from one game surface into another.

The historical record is uneven. Some groups left years of publications; others appear through Discord tags, event rosters, or scattered reporting. This page records what can be supported without turning a tag into membership or a self-description into independent proof of scale.

## Aephia and AEP

Aephia, Aephia Industries, and AEP are operator-confirmed names for the same guild/DAC. Funcracker and Prometheus are identified as co-founders. Aephia created one of the most substantial community documentary records through weekly newsletters, guides, tools, economic dashboards, interviews, event coverage, and onboarding material.

Its publications are first-party evidence for Aephia's own projects and aspirations, including its stated ambition to become a spacecraft manufacturer. Claims about influence, size, awards, or outcomes remain attributed unless independently corroborated. [Aephia Source Profile](../media/Aephia-Source-Profile.md)

MetaVerse was a member of AEP. That identity resolution should be used for discovery and historical context. No current affiliation is asserted because the retained evidence is historical rather than a recent, convergent membership signal.

## Agora

Agora describes itself as a collaborative guild centered on shared growth, continuous learning, organized information, coordinated logistics, and mutual support. The operator-supplied Portuguese guild statement names SAWYN and Neo_AArmstrong as leaders at the time of that statement.

The uppercase Greek lambda tag attached to a handle usually signals an Agora association. It does not by itself establish formal membership or current status. Agora's tag also must not be confused with The Vanguard's `VΛ`, which combines a Latin V with an uppercase Greek lambda.

The description is authoritative for Agora's own stated mission. Founding date, leadership continuity, membership scale, and later activity remain incomplete.

## Dark Matter

Dark Matter is operator-confirmed as a guild/DAC aligned with the Ustur faction. HNN reported the election of its first Dark Council in 2023, providing a dated glimpse of internal guild governance. The Dark Council is unrelated to the elected Star Atlas Council and to the lore Council of Peace.

Further work should recover the election record, roster, term, internal constitution, and subsequent organizational status.

## Rome

Rome appears as a visible guild/DAC in community records. Virtuwul is operator-confirmed as a Rome member and owner of the titan-class Rainbow Phi. That ownership attribution is community-qualified rather than an official ATMTA statement and should eventually be reconciled to dated chain evidence.

Rome's founding, leadership, factional alignment, projects, alliances, and current status require a dedicated evidence packet.

## The Vanguard

The Vanguard uses the display tag `VΛ`. A handle carrying that tag can support an association at the time of the captured message, but it cannot prove membership duration, authority, or continued affiliation. The guild should remain separate from Agora despite the shared lambda character.

## Other preserved identities

The master index also preserves The Club, Heimdall Industries, Quimera, the Intergalactic Coalition, and other organizations whose documentary histories remain thin. A name is enough to retain an entity for discovery; it is not enough to invent a chronology.

## Guilds as institutions

A mature history should distinguish:

- self-described mission and identity;
- observed public projects and event participation;
- member or leader claims at a dated moment;
- faction alignment;
- alliances and rivalries;
- governance participation;
- economic or technical products;
- dormancy, merger, reactivation, or dissolution.

## Membership evidence policy

The repository may infer current membership when the evidence is strong enough, but the inference must be explicit rather than silently converted into fact. Membership states are recorded as:

- `HISTORICAL_MEMBER` when a dated source or operator adjudication establishes past membership without current evidence;
- `CURRENT_MEMBER_CONFIRMED` when a recent roster, direct self-identification, or guild publication explicitly establishes membership;
- `CURRENT_MEMBERSHIP_INFERRED` when at least two recent, independent signals converge, such as persistent guild identification across separate captures plus guild-specific participation;
- `ASSOCIATION_ONLY` when a tag, appearance, or interaction supports association but not membership;
- `UNKNOWN` when the available context cannot distinguish the relationship.

Every current-state conclusion must state an `as_of` date, confidence, and the evidence used. A display tag alone, one old message, social proximity, or operator-confirmed historical membership is not enough for a current-membership inference. Leadership and office require direct evidence and are never inferred merely from membership.

Discord conversations may preserve persistent tensions among players or guilds when the conflict concerns Star Atlas activity and has enough context to be historically meaningful. Off-topic politics and unrelated personal attacks are excluded from knowledge evaluation.

On January 18, ATMTA General Counsel Dan Park confirmed in the Star Atlas Discord governance channel that the timeline for a Star Atlas Constitution and voting on proposals (PIPs) in the POLIS DAO has been “pushed back.” With things quiet on the governance front while the team continues building, I thought it would be good to take a look at the ways in which the SA community already governs itself. Public safety, community-built public utilities, and civil society have all emerged over the past two years and can be understood as emergent community self-government. Integrating these community-driven efforts into the formal architecture of the Star Atlas Constitution should be a priority for citizens of the SA metaverse, who long to exist in a truly sovereign digital nation-state.
Finally, there has been a substantial growth of civil society in the Star Atlas metaverse. The Hologram has led the way in independent SA journalism, while Metaverse Explorer and other YouTube channels provide virtual forums for Star Atlas interviews and discussion. As opinions and information circulate through various media outlets, Star Atlas citizens have the opportunity to get their updates directly from trusted, independent community members. Guilds and DACs are yet another form of emerging independent civil society, each with their own culture, identity, and self-government practices, where opinions and project info circulate independently from ATMTA moderation and visibility. The POLIS DAO should play in active role in fostering the growth of civil society in the metaverse, promoting citizens’ access to independent information and positive freedom of speech.
One of my hopes for the POLIS Constitution is that we can find ways to integrate already existing forms of community governance into the long-term architecture of the DAO. This will create a lasting acknowledgment of what the community has built so far and designate some areas where the DAO can begin playing a practical role.

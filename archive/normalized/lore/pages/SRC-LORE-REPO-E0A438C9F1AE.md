## The Architecture of the Neuno Region

> *"ONI is a choir of four voices singing in four languages. Beautiful — until you realize each voice has its own song."*
> — High Prelate Cassandra Vael, Church of MUD

The Neuno Region is unlike any other capital space in the galaxy. Where MUD's [Exodus Region](../geography/c4_lore_layer.md) radiates outward from a single human holy site, ONI's capital is a **constellation of cooperation** — each system reflecting a different facet of the four-species alliance.

At the center orbits **Neuno** itself — a 3,000-year-old space parasite fused with a planet's geological structure, whose immune system attacks its own inhabitants with tsunamis, droughts, and poisoned crops. The Mierese love it anyway. Surrounding this living world, the Central Space Station rotates its architectural style with each presiding species — currently displaying Mierese design under **Volmik Vaor Scarka's** leadership.

The CORE systems house the institutional backbone of the Consortium: cultural preservation, diplomatic infrastructure, and the administrative apparatus that keeps four species arguing productively. The SEC systems provide the economic and military foundation — tritium refineries, Punaab trade posts, Sogmian patrol routes, and classified Hikibashi installations that officially do not exist.

The region's resource profile is dominated by **Tritium Ore** — the fuel that powers warp travel and military fleets. Combined with substantial Aluminum, Hafnium, and Copper deposits, the Neuno Region is self-sufficient in the strategic minerals needed to sustain both the ONI fleet and the COP Cradle that Vaor Scarka commands.

## 👑 The Neuno System (CSS-ONI-KING-01)

> *"Neuno is not a home. Neuno is a relationship. A complicated, abusive, beautiful, irreplaceable relationship."*
> — Mierese saying

### Overview

The KING system of R-ONI-CSS is the gravitational and political center of the entire ONI Consortium. It contains the sentient planet **Neuno** — homeworld of the Mierese — and the **Central Space Station**, ONI's rotating headquarters. Every major decision affecting four species and seventeen territories passes through this system.

Neuno's radiant blue sun feeds the living planet through photosynthesis, driving cycles of lush growth and months-long hibernation. When Neuno sleeps, its verdant jungles become desolates of dust, its enormous animals shelter underground, and the pace of civilization slows to match the planet's breathing. When it wakes, the terrain morphs rapidly, reflecting the fluctuating temperaments of the sentient homeland — and the Mierese draw resilience from this ever-shifting landscape.

Neuno is a singularity — a sentient entity the size of a planet, born when a colossal space parasite collided with the world in the galaxy's primordial era and "infected" its geological structure. Growth originates from the southern pole, and the planet's biological immune system treats the Mierese as surface parasites, punishing expansion with natural disasters.

The planet is divided into **six ancestral regions**, each giving its Mierese inhabitants their third name:

| Region | Third Name | Character |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Singing Sands of Kaljura** | Jura | Desert dunes that produce harmonic tones — the most poetic Mierese |
| **Vireko Ravines** | Eko | Deep geological fissures with extreme biodiversity — the boldest explorers |
| **Aerqint Spires** | Qint | Towering rock formations piercing the atmosphere — the most isolated communities |
| **Morvalel Marsh** | Valel | Vast wetlands teeming with life — traditional heartland of the [Lore Keepers](../factions/mierese_lore_keepers.md) |
| **Umbyara Woods** | Yara | Dense, hostile jungles full of deadly predators — the toughest warriors |
| **Eskscarka Currents** | Scarka | Treacherous ocean currents — birthplace of the [Hikibashi](../factions/hikibashi.md) and Vaor Scarka |

The **Resonance Halls** — deep-ocean acoustic chambers where the geometry of stone and water creates extraordinary properties for vocal recitation — are the cultural heart of Mierese civilization. Here, Chief Lore Keeper **Ondara Valel** guards the Grand Tale's 47 Chapters, including the three Sealed Chapters whose contents are known only to her and two successors.

Neuno communicates through subtle sensations rather than words — only the wisest Lore Keepers attempt to interpret these feelings. When the Benevolent Explorers arrived in ~2377, they terraformed portions of the planet, advanced Mierese science, and left without explanation. Their identity remains one of the galaxy's great unsolved mysteries.

The **Central Space Station** is the headquarters of the ONI Consortium — a massive orbital installation whose architectural style rotates to reflect the currently presiding species. Under **Volmik Vaor Scarka's** Mierese leadership, the station displays flowing organic forms, bioluminescent lighting, and acoustic corridors designed for oral storytelling rather than written display. When the Sogmians next preside, the same corridors will be refaced in gray stone and Du Prah inscriptions.

The CSS houses the **Consortium Council Chambers** — the forum where all four species delegations negotiate policy. Major decisions require consensus across all four member civilizations, making ONI simultaneously the most representative and the most argumentative faction in the galaxy.

The station also serves as the primary COP liaison point for the ONI Safe Zone and as a commercial exchange managed by Punaab merchants. Diplomatic delegations from MUD, the Ustur Regency, and neutral institutions maintain permanent quarters — making the CSS the most species-diverse single installation in sovereign space.

Morvalim serves as the **off-world extension** of the Lore Keepers' cultural infrastructure — a secondary archive world where young Keepers train in the art of memorization and recitation. Unlike Neuno's Resonance Halls, which are sacred and restricted, Morvalim's facilities are accessible to ONI citizens of all species who wish to hear public chapters of the Grand Tale.

The planet houses the secondary **Annual Assembly Site** — the location where elder Lore Keepers reunite each Neuno year to share collected stories and vote on which should be incorporated into the Grand Tale. The primary site remains on Neuno, in an unknown location that even the Hikibashi has not confirmed.

A Sogmian Honor Guard maintains a permanent barracks on Morvalim, providing security for the cultural infrastructure at the personal request of the Kur Faists — a gesture that embodies the Du Prah principle: *"provide shelter for those who seek it."* The Lore Keepers tolerate the guards. Oreli is, on official maps, a **Mierese oceanic research station** — an unremarkable Type-5 world with subsurface water reserves and modest mineral deposits. Its population records list approximately 800,000 marine researchers and their support staff.

In reality, Oreli is one of the [Hikibashi's](../factions/hikibashi.md) primary training installations. Its deep ocean zones simulate the treacherous **Eskscarka Currents** of Neuno — the birthplace of the Three Currents doctrine. Recruits undergo **The Drowning** here: the Hikibashi's selection program, conducted in extreme marine environments where the graduation rate is fifteen percent.

The Hikibashi's existence is **officially denied** at every level of Mierese government. Oreli's true purpose is known only to the Commandant, Vaor Scarka, and the forty to sixty operatives who survived its ocean floors.

Named in tribute to the Mierese tradition of multi-layered cooperation, this CORE system houses the permanent delegations of all four ONI species and the bureaucratic apparatus that keeps the Consortium functional.

## 🟢 The Eskri System (CSS-ONI-SEC-01) — The Current's Edge

### Overview

The SEC-01 system is the **primary industrial zone** of the Neuno Region — where the strategic minerals that sustain ONI's fleet and economy are extracted and processed. Named for the Eskscarka Currents (shortened to Eskri in common usage), the system's identity reflects the Mierese region that produced both the Hikibashi and Vaor Scarka himself.

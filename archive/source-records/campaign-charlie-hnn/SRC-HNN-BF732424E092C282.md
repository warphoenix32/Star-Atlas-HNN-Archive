Reviewing the New Ogrika Tursic: Underpowered Elegance
Posted by Cpt. August Wulff | Jun 4, 2022 | Gameplay
The newest addition to the Medium Fighter category, the Ogrika Tursic, has been made available on the Galactic Marketplace today after a brief delay. Originating for 2,475 USDC, the ship is 25 USDC cheaper than the origination price of the Pearce F4, and 230 USDC more expensive than the Calico ATS Enforcer. The Tursic is the first Epic rarity Medium fighter, above the Rare PF4 and Uncommon Enforcer. Do the Tursic’s stats justify its rarity and origination price? Let’s break it down.
Disclaimer: This review was created in advance of full gameplay statistics and in-game performance data for the ship(s) in question. Ship characteristics discussed in this article, such as components, modules, and crew, are not finalized and may be subject to change in the future. As such, any conclusions reached by the author are of an inherently speculative nature, and should not be considered financial advice.
Underwhelming Armaments
Previously, I have criticized Ogrika for giving their Small fighter undersized missiles and defensive components. However, the Mik compensates for this with a lower origination price. The Tursic suffers from a similar problem, without the discounted price: while the other Medium fighters have two Medium missile bays, those on the Tursic are both Small. Undersized missiles could majorly reduce the Tursic’s burst damage. For sustained damage, the Tursic has four Medium weapon hardpoints. Compared to its peers, both of which have at least one oversized weapon, the Tursic seems to be at a disadvantage in terms of effective range. It’s hard to foresee how DPS will compare between (for example) four Medium and two Large guns, but the Tursic is unlikely to stand out in combat, the main duty for fighters.
The rest of the Tursic’s components are unremarkable: Medium hull reinforcement, Medium warp drive, Medium power supply, and so on. For an Epic ship with some components below standard size, one might expect to see conspicuous advantages in other areas, but these are difficult to identify. Sizing up the crew, the Tursic does have a full complement of five, including captain, pilot, co-pilot, engineer, and turret gunner, similar to the PF4. This gives the Tursic at least one advantage over the two-person Enforcer.
As for modules, the Tursic has a Medium fuel tank, like the other two Medium fighters, as well as a Medium mess hall. What advantages does the mess hall provide over extra ammunition or cargo storage? We can only speculate, but the ship’s description emphasizes crew comfort. Perhaps there will be a bonus to crew morale, which could play a significant role in the Tursic’s performance. The mess hall may also more efficiently utilize the Food resource, given that storing bulk ingredients for cooking is generally easier than storing ready-to-eat rations.
Ogrika Tursic Interior – Official Art by ATMTA, Inc.
Potential Strengths and Best-Fit Role
Although I would place the Tursic at the bottom of a hypothetical Medium fighter power ranking, it’s important to acknowledge the strengths and potential uses for every new ship in the catalog. First of all, it’s one of the most beautiful ships in the game in my opinion, another elegant elaboration of the Punaab style. The Ogrika Tursic evokes a space-age fusion of Rococo and Art Deco aesthetics, with wings and tail like an Earthling bird-of-paradise. Richly ornamented both inside and out, I expect players to spend plenty of time appreciating this ship in the Showroom. The Tursic gets full marks on style from me; it even has a chandelier in the mess hall.
As for gameplay/mechanical strengths, these are harder to assess. The ship’s description mentions that the elaborate wings give it increased maneuverability “in atmosphere or in space.” It will be interesting to compare the performance of fighters in dogfight scenarios in both environments, with the devs dropping hints that atmospheric and space conditions will be significantly different. Pilots may need to select their ship carefully depending on their area of operations. The components don’t suggest outstanding ability in any one area, but a well-rounded crew complement is sure to be an asset. With fuel and mess hall modules to sustain the ship and personnel for extended periods, in my estimation the Tursic is best-suited to long-haul patrol missions. However, it may underperform its peers in set-piece engagements.
Pros:
-Stunning looks inside and out
-Well-equipped for long-distance patrols
-Crew of five in a variety of roles
Cons:
-Undersized missile hardpoints
-No oversized components or modules to note
-High origination price relative to assessed firepower
Best-Fit Role: long-range escort & patrol missions

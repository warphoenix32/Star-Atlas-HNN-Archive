# ONI Consortium

The **ONI Consortium** is the major multi-species faction of the Galia Expanse. Its founding story is not one of cultural uniformity. It is the story of different peoples who entered an alliance because isolation had become more dangerous than cooperation.

The captured lore source identifies four peoples as central to the consortium: the **Sogmian**, **Punaab**, **Photoli**, and **Mierese**. Each brings its own traditions, institutions, and interests. ONI is therefore a political coalition, not a species and not a single culture.

## An alliance forged in exile

The source places ONI's origin in the crisis of the [Convergence War](Convergence-War.md). Sogmian intervention on behalf of the Tufa brought devastating retaliation, including the assassination of their leader and the near-destruction of their society. Survivors withdrew into high-risk space.

The later alliance joined Sogmian military discipline with Punaab commercial networks, Photoli knowledge, and Mierese mobility and storytelling traditions. The source presents the coalition as a practical response to near-extinction: peoples with different strengths built a common faction because none could safely face the conflict alone.

This origin is essential to ONI's identity. Cooperation is not represented as sentimental harmony. It is an institution built under pressure, sustained by mutual dependency, memory, and negotiation.

## Four peoples, several political traditions

The consortium's diversity is its strength and its permanent political problem. The source portrays:

- the **Sogmian** through honor, discipline, martial preparation, and the binding principles of the Du Prah;
- the **Punaab** through trade, wealth, and commercial reach;
- the **Photoli** through ancient knowledge, energy, and a society that lacks a single central government; and
- the **Mierese** through movement, memory, oral history, improvisation, and extensive networks.

These summaries are not substitutes for the separate species histories. They explain why ONI cannot be modeled as a unitary nation. Its member peoples retain identities that predate or exceed the consortium.

## War, the Last Stand, and political settlement

The captured faction page associates ONI's formation with a decisive wartime effort and the construction of a Titan-class vessel called **The Last Stand**. It credits the coalition with helping force the war toward an end and with becoming a co-founder of the [Council of Peace](Council-of-Peace.md).

The source also attributes commercial, military, intelligence, and cultural institutions to the consortium. These organizations should remain separate entities. A covert service, trade network, cultural order, vessel, or central station can operate within ONI without becoming an alias for it.

Within the Council of Peace, ONI is associated with a rotating executive role in the captured narrative. Specific leaders and rotations are snapshot claims. The durable point is that ONI participates as one of three major factional powers in the postwar institutional order.

## Cooperation and secrecy

ONI's narrative repeatedly contrasts its public ideal of cooperation with the secrecy required to protect a coalition born during war. Honor traditions coexist with intelligence operations. Open trade networks coexist with institutions designed to conceal information. A faction founded by refugees can become a major power whose security apparatus worries allies as well as rivals.

This tension gives ONI a distinct place in Star Atlas lore. It is neither the dynastic-religious civilization represented by MUD nor the awakened philosophical civilization represented by Ustur. It is an alliance that must continually persuade several peoples that shared power is better than separation.

## Evidence boundaries

The Archive accepts the lore repository's ONI name and faction classification, and it uses the source as the principal canonical snapshot for this history. The faction's member peoples remain separate canonical entities.

Claims about exact survivor counts, military precedence, current officeholders, intelligence penetration, territorial holdings, or comparative galactic rank are retained as source assertions. They should not be presented as independently verified or permanently current without corroborating official publications.

## Current interpretation

ONI is best understood as a multi-species political project forged during existential crisis. Its canonical identity depends on the continued distinctness of its peoples: cooperation is meaningful precisely because Sogmian, Punaab, Photoli, and Mierese histories are not merged into one.

The captured source strongly supports that institutional narrative. Exact present leadership, territorial reach, and operational claims remain qualified until reconciled with later official lore.

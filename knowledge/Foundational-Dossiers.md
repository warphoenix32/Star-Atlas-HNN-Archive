# Foundational Knowledge Dossiers

Star Atlas has accumulated years of announcements, proposals, games, economic systems, community broadcasts, and in-universe history. The ten dossiers below provide a way into that record without asking a reader to begin with a registry or a database.

Each dossier has one primary narrative and a smaller set of supporting research pages. The narrative explains the history; the supporting pages preserve the detailed states, ledgers, and unresolved evidence that make the account auditable.

## 1. How Star Atlas governance took shape

Begin with [Governance Constitutional History](governance/Governance-Constitutional-History.md). It follows the institutional model from the first captured DAO framework through the later roles of the Foundation and elected Council.

Continue with the [Star Atlas DAO](governance/Star-Atlas-DAO.md), [Star Atlas Foundation](governance/Star-Atlas-Foundation.md), and [Star Atlas Council](governance/Star-Atlas-Council.md) when the distinction among electorate, implementation body, and process steward matters. The central caution is structural: a DAO vote does not itself perform implementation, and no shared Star Atlas identity makes these institutions interchangeable.

## 2. Proposals, elections, failures, and withdrawals

The [Governance Failure and Termination Casebook](governance/Governance-Failure-and-Termination-Casebook.md) is the best narrative entry point for understanding why a governance history cannot be reduced to passed and failed votes. It separates rejection, supersession, post-passage termination, cancellation, withdrawal, and missing implementation evidence.

Use the [PIP Registry](governance/PIP-Registry.md) for proposal-level facts and the [Council Election History](governance/Council-Election-History.md) for mechanism-aware election results. Candidate advancement is not final election, and passage is not execution.

## 3. Treasury authority and the limits of payment evidence

[DAO Treasury Architecture](economy/DAO-Treasury-Architecture.md) explains the wider institutional and account system without collapsing it into the specifically named DAO Treasury account. The [Treasury Authorization and Payment Ledger](economy/Treasury-Authorization-and-Payment-Ledger.md) then records requests, votes, authorizations, Council reports, and missing transaction evidence as separate states.

The [Ecosystem Fund Award History](governance/Ecosystem-Fund-Award-History.md) applies the same method to program awards. An authorized award, a reported milestone, a payment assertion, an on-chain transfer, and a verified outcome require different evidence.

## 4. ATLAS, POLIS, and the measured economy

Read [ATLAS Token History](economy/ATLAS-Token-History.md) and [POLIS Token History](economy/POLIS-Token-History.md) as two related but distinct institutional histories. ATLAS has been described across exchange, reward, cost, and payment contexts; POLIS supports governance and locking. Neither token should be defined only by one historical program.

The [Economic Report Catalog](economy/Economic-Report-Catalog.md) preserves the official quarterly research series and its methodological limits. The reports establish what the publisher measured and reported at a given time, not an independent audit or an automatically comparable time series.

## 5. From SCORE to SAGE Labs and Starbased

[SCORE and Faction Fleet](gameplay/SCORE-and-Faction-Fleet.md) explains the early browser surface and emissions program. [SAGE Product-Family History](gameplay/SAGE.md) follows later named products and stages while keeping SAGE Labs, Starbased, SAGE 3D, and C4 distinct.

The [Product Registry](gameplay/Product-Registry.md) provides the narrow lifecycle view. A family name can remain active while a particular program ends, a browser surface changes, or a successor remains only planned.

## 6. The UE5 Showroom and Holosim

The [UE5 Showroom History](gameplay/UE5-Showroom.md) documents the pre-alpha Unreal Engine client and named updates. The [Holosim Release and Feature History](gameplay/Holosim.md) covers its public test, seasons, delays, and browser-facing capabilities.

These histories preserve the difference among demonstration, testing, public availability, update, and completion of a wider roadmap. They should not be treated as interchangeable versions of one product.

## 7. How Star Atlas communicated development

The [Official Communications Chronology](timeline/Official-Communications-Chronology.md) is the narrative spine. It connects dated publication surfaces without treating an announcement as proof that the announced event later occurred.

The source profiles for [Medium](media/Star-Atlas-Medium-Publication-Profile.md), [Discord announcements](media/Official-Discord-Announcements-Profile.md), and [the official X account](media/Official-X-Account-Profile.md) explain what each preserved corpus contains, how it was captured, and where discovery remains incomplete.

## 8. Atlas Brew, Town Halls, and the recorded community

[Atlas Brew History](media/Atlas-Brew-History.md) explains the community broadcast and its preserved transcript corpus. [Star Atlas Town Hall History](media/Star-Atlas-Town-Hall-History.md) covers the official live-discussion format. The [VBTV / Star Atlas TV Recording Index](media/VBTV-Star-Atlas-TV-Recording-Index.md) keeps the broadcaster and recording inventory distinct.

Unknown speakers do not erase the value of timestamped evidence. They do limit named quotation and institutional attribution, which is why these pages emphasize episode identity, date, timestamp, and context.

## 9. The institutions and people behind Star Atlas

The [Institutional Overview](organizations/Institutional-Overview.md) is the entry point for ATMTA, the DAO, Foundation, Council, and associated programs. [ATMTA](organizations/ATMTA.md) addresses the operating company specifically, while the [Organization and Role Registry](organizations/Organization-and-Role-Registry.md) preserves dated roles and unresolved identity history.

This dossier distinguishes historical roles from current ones. A person or organization may appear in several parts of the record without holding the same authority in every period.

## 10. The Galia Expanse

[Galia Expanse](lore/Galia-Expanse.md) introduces the setting. The three major faction histories—[Manus Ultima Divina](lore/Manus-Ultima-Divina.md), [ONI Consortium](lore/ONI-Consortium.md), and [Ustur](lore/Ustur.md)—lead into the [Council of Peace](lore/Council-of-Peace.md), the [Convergence War](lore/Convergence-War.md), and the [Peoples of Galia](lore/Peoples-of-Galia.md).

The [Canon Registry](lore/Canon-Registry.md) remains the authority and continuity guide. These narratives use the ATMTA-affiliated lore repository's preferred taxonomy, preserve older names as aliases, and identify the captured source as a historical snapshot rather than a guarantee of current completeness.

## What comes next

These dossiers complete the Knowledge consolidation gate. They are the evidence-qualified foundation for a later publication layer, where a smaller portfolio can be rewritten as elegant public articles. Publication drafting must preserve the limits recorded here; it cannot create stronger certainty by simplifying the prose.

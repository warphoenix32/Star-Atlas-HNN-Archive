After an extended period of vacancy, the Dark Council has been fully nominated and confirmed by the general members of the Pirate Guild Dark Matter.
Press enter or click to view image in full size
Dark Matter, the Ustur pirate guild, recently concluded their elections for Dark Council. This is the first time in Dark Matter history that the entire council has been filled.
Voting was held this past Monday and every nominee for Dark Council received the minimum number of votes to become confirmed. The new ranking members of the Dark Council are:
Signal
CapThor
Pock the Pirate
Fury
These four council members join guild leader Chaldan to comprise the Dark Council. Previously, a council position was held by Cosmos who stepped down prior to elections.
Elections were conducted using a public polling system, with a dedicated chat thread for members to share their opinions and views on matters related to the elections and council duties.
Each position in the council will have specific responsibilities, including PR, community management, recruitment, internal tool development, generating resources for the guild, and more. However, finalization of council duties was still in progress.
Council Sets Broad Vision
The new council members have set goals to increase the public presence of Dark Matter through a strong social media presence, participation in Star Atlas events, forming alliances with players and guilds who share similar values, and promoting the advancement of active guild members in their areas of interest.
The council aims to organize “hacker house” like events to foster innovation in the Star Atlas ecosystem, provide resources for community members to grow in their desired areas, create roles for advancing active members in future gameplay experiences, and establish an effective system for distributing guild resources based on participation.
One of the main tasks of the new council is to draft a governance paper or constitution that will serve as a foundation for the guild’s future.
Dark Matter’s goal is to work on developing all the necessary tools to become a fully decentralized and democratic guild. Additionally, the guild aims to become a politically influential entity within the Ustur faction, aligning its interests and activities accordingly.
Dark Matter Prepares for SAGE
Dark Matter has a dedicated community of 30 to 40 active members, all active and eagerly awaiting more complete versions of SAGE. The guild is currently discussing the implementation of term limits, following the “one person, one vote” rule, and future elections will stagger the refreshment of 2 to 3 seats at a time instead of all 5 positions at once. Sufficient time will be given to members for voting to ensure that everyone’s voice is heard and democracy is upheld.

The first annual Star Atlas Joni Awards are just around the corner and with them comes The Hologram’s first ever prediction list for the event.
If you haven't voted yet, you can do so here. Read and vote before Dec 14th.
Let’s get started.

1. Community Member of the Year: Funcracker
Twitter
Funcracker has been, undoubtedly, a pillar of the Star Atlas community. As a co-founder of Aephia Industries, Funcracker has helped guide many Star Atlassians along their journey.
2. Most Knowledgeable Community Member: Funcracker
Funcracker isn’t just well liked by the community for his personality. His knowledge of many of Star Atlas’s undocumented mechanics is almost second to none. Whether its town halls, Atlas Brews, or Twitter Spaces, Funcracker is always tuned in to the Star Atlas know how.
While there are many in the community who could give Funcracker a run for his money, of the nominees, he takes the cake.
3. Community Helper of the Year: Odvb
As a Star Atlas Discord Community Mod, it’s no surprise that Odvb is a final contended. A constant presence in the server, Odvb can always be found tirelessly point new members in the right direction.
4. Small Guild of the Year: Scythe
Of the small guild nominees, there’s only one true distinct winner and that is Scythe Guild. Over the past 6 months, Scythe has managed to raise $1,200 in donations from the Star Atlas community towards noble causes like cancer research. One of the few charitable guilds in the community, Scythe brings philanthropy to Star Atlas.

5. Medium Guild of the Year: Rome
Rome guild has managed to build one of the most robust presences in the metaverse to date. Rome guild members are veterans of the crypto and metaverse space, with key members hosting a virtual talk show every Sunday to discuss the latest metaverse news.

6. Large Guild of the Year: Quimera
If there’s one defining force of Star Atlas, it’s Quimera. Hosting their own Quimercon in-person event, Quimera is one of the largest guilds in Star Atlas. While they may appear absent in the English section of the Star Atlas discord, Quimera is a mainstay of the Spanish channels. While the English community remains split across various guilds, Quimera has managed to unite a vast majority of the Spanish community under one banner.

7. Best Guild Website: Aephia.com
Replete with a repository of information, Aephia.com has a robust array of Star Atlas information in a simple, easy to read format. The various tools on the website help new Aephia members find the information they need quickly.
8. Best Content Creator: Morpheus
A leader in the French Star Atlas community, Morpheus produces some of the most creative Star Atlas content in any language. Despite being a French speaking content creator, the name Morpheus is recognized far and wide.
9. Best Video: How to Play Star Atlas by DAOCaster
DAOCaster was one of the original content creators for Star Atlas and was critical in onboarding likely thousands of new members, including this writer. His coverage of the emerging metaverse that is Star Atlas inspired many to take their first steps into blockchain gaming.

11. Community Created Project of the Year: Redspatula
One of the most visited websites for Star Atlas ship market data, Redspatula is a critical piece of information for ship buyers and market watchers. Their site has helped countless players find the best deals on ships at the right price.

12. Favorite Solana Project: Phantom
Phantom is one of the most visible projects on the Solana ecosystem and key to decentralized finance management. As a key integrator to Star Atlas, Phantom will undoubtedly see huge potential and growth alongside Star Atlas.

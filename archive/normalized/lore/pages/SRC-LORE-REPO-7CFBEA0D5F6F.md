## Overview

Pavo Passage was once the jewel of a single dynasty — now it's the galaxy's most glamorous den of vipers. Strategically positioned at the **frontier of Ustur and MUD territories**, this commerce hub hides a web of **clandestine alliances** and **covert power plays** beneath a glittering exterior. The **Order of Seasons** — assassins who secretly revere the Iris aspect **Dusk** — lurk in its shadows.

The sector is governed by the **Ruling Conclave**, an oligarchic council disguised as democracy, operating from the **Gilded Citadel** on Cathris. Real power flows through the **Toll Authority of Pavo Passage (TAPP)** — a bureaucratic machine that converts transit into sovereignty.

## History

### Rise: Warlord Pavo & the Crumon Dynasty

- **Warlord Pavo** (Crumon species) forged dynastic dominance along the MUD-Ustur highway
- Transformed Pavo Passage into a **toll-powered realm**
- The Dynasty's leverage grew by **playing outside powers against each other**
- Strategic position at the border drove explosive commercial growth
- **~2463**: The Pavo Royal Family starts establishing relationships with both **MUD and Ustur** factions, using them to gain leverage and grow Pavo Passage's strength
- **~2513**: During the Convergence War, the Pavo royal family **consistently changes sides**, causing the emergence of both MUD and Ustur resentment. Both factions begin **supporting the Pavos' political adversaries** to overthrow their ruling

### The Night (Sector-Defining Event)
>
> [!CAUTION]
> **SECRET**: The truth about "The Night" is the most closely guarded secret in Pavo Passage.

- **Public knowledge**: The entire Crumon royal family, including **Queen Cathris**, vanished in a single night. The event remains officially unresolved.
- **The secret truth**: **~2521**: As opposition to the royal family grows, the Pavo royal family **contracted the Order of Seasons** (from the HRZ) — a group of mysterious assassins that claims to worship Iris — to assassinate political adversaries.
- **~2522-09**: In **one year and one month**, the Order of Seasons fulfilled their contract — **slaughtering all opposition party leaders** in the sector.
- **The Night**: When the royals **failed to pay** for services rendered, the Order retaliated by killing the entire royal family. Only **Ike Pavo** survived — he was a baby, not present in the sector.
- **Political aftermath**: Everyone strongly suspects the Order, but nobody speaks of it openly — fear keeps the truth buried. The Conclave uses "The Night" as justification for permanent "emergency doctrine."

### Modern Era

- The Dynasty collapsed; power vacuumed into the hands of competing elites
- **~2565**: **Civil war erupts** in Pavo Passage — **Crumon loyalists** fight to restore dynastic rule against the rest of the sector's factions. The loyalists ultimately **surrender** to preserve scraps of power
- **~2567**: The political groups of Pavo create a **façade of democracy** to formally end the civil war — the **Ruling Conclave** — splitting power amongst the various factions
- The sector became a battleground of **soft influence** between MUD (via MTC/Synod) and Ustur (via Regency)

### Post-War Expansion

- **~2524**: With both **MUD and Ustur exhausted** from the Convergence War, neither can pressure Pavo any longer. The sector **thrives** in the power vacuum and begins **spreading its influence across the Medium Risk Zone**

## The Toll Machine (TAPP)

*(Full details: `canon/factions/toll_authority_of_pavo_passage.md`)*

The sector runs on a 4-stage toll system: **Classify → Authorize → Route & Protect → Settle**. No faction controls all four stages.

**Anti-monopoly rule**: No company may hold >3 active routes while the competitor has <1.

## The Balance of Power

The sector exists in **managed imbalance**:

- **MTC** tries to make Pavo depend on MUD/Synod contracts
- **VHE** keeps an alternative alive with Ustur backing
- **Crumon Dynasty** plays both sides while rebuilding leverage
- **Ustur Regency** audits and standardizes without occupying
- **Winter Fist Path** deters military escalation
- **Order of Seasons** ensures nobody wins too cleanly
- **The Conclave** distributes blame and manages workflow

> *"In Pavo Passage, success looks like: nothing burning today."*

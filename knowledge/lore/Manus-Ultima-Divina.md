# Manus Ultima Divina (MUD)

**Manus Ultima Divina**, or **MUD**, is the human major faction of the Galia Expanse. Its source history combines survival, religious conviction, dynastic authority, scientific ambition, military command, and commercial expansion. Humanity's escape from a dying Earth is remembered not simply as migration, but as the founding ordeal that gave MUD its institutions and its belief in a chosen destiny.

The Archive treats *Manus Ultima Divina* as the preferred faction name and **MUD** as its established abbreviation. The faction must remain distinct from the human species, individual MUD governments, corporations, military bodies, and citizens.

## From Earth to the Mothership Serene

The captured lore source places MUD's formation on Earth around 2120. In its account, the world faced a civilization-ending meteor while a stable society had lost the capacity to innovate rapidly enough to escape. Vincenzo Assis Gotti became the organizing figure of a movement dedicated to survival through accelerated development.

MUD theology remembers a divine manifestation that warned Gotti of the coming disaster. The same source introduces a deeper interpretation involving Ahr, a Photoli traveler, while emphasizing that ordinary MUD belief understands the visitor as God. The distinction is part of the fiction's layered perspective: theological truth inside MUD society and the source's wider cosmology are not necessarily the same account.

The movement consumed enormous resources to build the **Mothership Serene** and leave Earth. The source presents this achievement as both salvation and moral injury. MUD survived, but the methods of survival deepened a lasting conflict with ECOS and established a pattern in which collective destiny could be used to justify concentrated power.

## Settlement and institutional power

After reaching Galia, MUD became a political civilization rather than only an exodus fleet. The source associates its settled order with the Gotti dynasty and with several internal centers of power:

- the **Church**, which guards the faction's religious worldview;
- the **Pearce Council**, associated with military command;
- **Scientia**, associated with knowledge and research;
- the **Synod**, associated with commerce and economic influence; and
- the **Senate**, representing a political institution whose power is portrayed as constrained.

These bodies should not be collapsed into MUD itself. They describe competing institutional forces within the faction. The source's central political tension is therefore internal as well as external: faith, military security, research, commerce, and deliberative authority do not always point in the same direction.

## The Convergence War and the Council of Peace

MUD participated in the conflict remembered as the [Convergence War](Convergence-War.md). The captured narrative attributes colonial aggression and later retreat from medium-risk territories to the faction, while also making MUD one of the powers that accepted the settlement ending the war.

MUD then became a founding member of the [Council of Peace](Council-of-Peace.md). The source associates it with the Council's legislative function and a rotating balance among the three major factions. Because officeholders and rotations can change, the Archive treats specific "current" leadership language as belonging to the captured snapshot rather than timeless canon.

## Identity, ambition, and contradiction

MUD's history is written around a productive contradiction. The faction's conviction in human purpose supports technological achievement, coordination, and survival. The same conviction can become a rationale for dominion, extraction, and political control.

Its lore also connects spiritual and commercial power. MUD is not depicted only as a theocracy or only as a market empire; the Synod, Church, dynasty, military, and scientific institutions form a system in which influence can move through belief, debt, security, and technological dependency.

This makes MUD more than the "human faction." It is a specific historical answer to the question of what humanity became after Earth: a civilization that interprets survival as proof of destiny and remains divided over how that destiny should be used.

## Evidence boundaries

The ATMTA-affiliated lore repository is authoritative here for the preferred name, faction classification, and captured internal taxonomy. It is also a strong canonical-source snapshot for the faction's narrative.

It does not establish that every leadership title, fleet ranking, territorial count, or quantitative claim remains current. Earlier marketing material and later official lore may use narrower descriptions or reveal a different publication stage. Historical source wording should remain preserved even when a later preferred term changes.

## Current interpretation

The strongest defensible synthesis is that MUD is a human factional civilization born from Earth's existential crisis, organized through the Gotti legacy and several competing institutions, and later transformed by settlement, war, and participation in the Council of Peace.

The details of present leadership and territorial power require later-source reconciliation. The historical themes—survival, divine mandate, institutional rivalry, commercial reach, and the moral cost of ambition—are central to the captured canonical narrative.

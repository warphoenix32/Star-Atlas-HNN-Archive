## Preserved Official Text

Before you can use your ships and resources in C4 PTR, you need to import them into the game through the Port of Entry.
In PTR, assets are provided for testing purposes. These are not real assets
Step 1: Open the Port of Entry
After creating your C4 PTR profile, you will arrive on the main map.
To start importing your assets:
Look at the bottom-right corner of the map.
Click the Port of Entry.
The Port of Entry is where you can move your provided PTR assets into the game.
Step 2: Select the assets you want to import
Inside the import interface, you will see the available items that can be imported.
To select your assets:
Click MAX on the right side of each item you want to import.
Click Deposit next to each selected item.
This will move the selected items into the manifest on the right side of the screen.
Step 3: Review the transfer manifest
After selecting your assets, review the manifest on the right side of the interface.
The manifest shows which assets are ready to be transferred into the game.
Before continuing, make sure the items you want to import are listed there.
Step 4: Transfer the manifest
Once your manifest is ready:
Click Transfer Manifest at the top of the manifest box.
Wait for the transfer to complete.
After the transfer is complete, your assets will be imported into your faction CSS.
Where are imported assets sent?
Imported assets are sent to your faction CSS.
The CSS is the only base that has a Port of Entry for import and export.
Important notes
PTR assets are provided for testing and are not real assets.
You need to import your assets before you can use them in C4 PTR.
The import process in PTR is expected to be similar to the mainnet process, although the PTR remains a test environment and may change over time.
Your imported assets will be available from your faction CSS.
What to do next
Once your assets are imported, you can start using them in C4 PTR.
Recommended next steps:
Learn how to use the C4 PTR map.
Review the main menus.
Create or manage fleets.
Start exploring the available PTR systems.

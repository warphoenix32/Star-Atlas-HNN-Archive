Star Atlas Community Reacts Critically to StarPath Program
Posted by Krigs | Aug 11, 2022 | Community, Gameplay
A few weeks have passed since the release of Star Atlas’ referral system known as the StarPath program. Recently, the program has come under critical review by community members voicing concerns over the program’s stringent requirements, poor new player on ramping process, and marginal rewards.
On August 9th, popular Star Atlas talk show Metaverse Nomads discussed the critical failures of the program. The show also featured Funcracker, Co-Founder of Aephia Industries.
Show host Witticus, who is a veteran of the internet traffic and advertising industry, opened the discussion with a some volleyed comments about the program.
For 99.99% of people in the Star Atlas community there’s no reason to even look at Tier 2 or beyond because that is not where anyone, most people, are going to be. Most people are not going to refer $25,000 worth of ship sales to Star Atlas
Witticus is of course referring to the the three reward tiers of the StarPath program. To achieve Tier 2, a referrer must secure $25,000 in qualified sales. For Tier 3, that number jumps to an even $100,000.
To qualify as a referred sale, the referee must purchase directly from the Star Atlas seller account at Origination Prices. Secondary sales do not count as credit towards qualified sales. Secondary sales are defined as transactions that occur between two players on the market. However, due to current market conditions, secondary sales are vastly cheaper in price, meaning a new player would have to intentionally purchase a higher priced ship. This is divergent from the idea of rewarding free market principles.
Witticus continued by highlighting the low return on rewards compared to industry standards for paid traffic.
A user would need to refer a enough users to purchase 50 Fimbul Airbikes, at 20 dollars each, to qualify for one XX-Small ship reward. This amounts to a 2.0% to as low as .038% return on traffic value.
Witticus ended his portion of the segment with a plea to the StarPath program’s development team.
If you want us to be brand ambassadors, be fair to us.
Funcracker continued the panel by adding in that Star Atlas is considering adding additional rewards to the program to possible include increased payouts in prosperity marks. The team [Star Atlas] has received the recent feedback and is considering updating the StarPath program in the future.
Commenting on the recent StarPath figures disclosed at the most recent Star Atlas Town Hall, Funcracker stated that he was not blown away by the number of people that had presently signed up. Adding onto the discussion, Witticus opined that he was not surprised either.
In the audience, Star Atlas YouTuber OCG Thor commented that he did not expect any personal rewards from the program.
Possible Game Imbalance Due to Faction Rewards
Towards the end of the show, panel member FancyHat discussed that the leading faction in the StarPath program would receive a Star Base defense boost for that quarter. In traditional MMOs, defending control points is already an advantage. Adding in defense bonuses for an entire quarter could put the disadvantageous factions on the back foot for a three month period.
A referral program in any game is critical to the influx of new members to sustain future growth. As it currently sits, the StarPath program provides little incentive for community members to effectively and efficiently onboard new players. Furthermore, the steps needed to satisfy program requirements put significant burdens on both recruiters and new players. Speaking broadly about the Star Atlas marketing campaign, a more concerted effort might be needed if the goal remain 1 million players by year’s end.

Conversation around Star Atlas Council heats up
Posted by FowlAC | Oct 5, 2022 | DAO, Politics
Only days after Star Atlas announced its Sustainable Governance Framework whitepaper, some “devoted” community members have already started to announce their candidacies for the Star Atlas Council. As of Tuesday, [DARK] Signal, Bodhi [TREE] and [EMP] Jith Blade have all officially declared with a few others still on the fence.
For those unfamiliar, here’s how Star Atlas is describing the Star Atlas Council:
The decisions of the Star Atlas DAO shall be guided, but not controlled, by a Council of elected representatives. We believe that the quality of decision making by a large community is improved by having a council shepherd the proposal ideation and drafting process such that proposals will receive a full vetting before formally being submitted for voting. The Star Atlas Council will provide input on draft proposals, with an eye for potential legal pitfalls and malicious or unintended effects. Star Atlas Council members will be elected and replaced by the Star Atlas DAO via on-chain governance.
For the time being, Star Atlas is being vague on specifics, instead saying:
The number of Council members and other details regarding the Star Atlas Council (such as the duration of their term) will be determined with input from the community. We encourage highly active and devoted members of the community to run for seats on the Star Atlas Council.
On that note, the “Governance” channel in the Star Atlas Discord was exploding with suggestions and strong opinions – mainly from the usual group of vocal active community members. Hundreds of messages have been posted since the channel launched on October 3.
The Hologram scanned through the entire channel to capture the overall general sentiment. Major themes:
Term limits
Size of Council
Requirements and qualifications for becoming a Council member
Power and influence of the Council
Compensation for Council members
Bad actors (e.g., prevention and removal of)
Role of Council vs. Role of Star Atlas Foundation
As expected, there was little consensus on any of the issues discussed. The most heated was the issue of compensation. Some felt that Council members needed to be paid for their time. Doing so would increase the pool and diversity of the applicants as well as increase accountability. Others felt it should be an unpaid position to ensure fairness and discourage bad actors from applying for Council spots. Another interesting discussion centered around Council qualifications. Should one be required to have significant holdings of Star Atlas assets, especially Polis? Also, would one need to disclose those assets and any other conflicts of interest as a member of the Council?

The only thing that is crystal clear at this point is that this is still early days. In other words, what we’re seeing right now is just a giant exercise in “theory crafting.” That said, there is certainly a high level of interest in how Star Atlas will be governed with many in the community seeing politics as perhaps the most important and “powerful” role one can play in Star Atlas. However, today, Aephia co-founder Funcracker, described what he thinks is a more plausible role of a Council member:
I still see people talking about control, or these council members wielding too much influence. The way I get it is that people who volunteer for this role will have very little influence on the whole voting process. To me it seems that they are basically performing some kind of paralegal duties, which involves reading through each PIP carefully in order to assess if there are any potential issues with it (aka vetting). Additionally, they will all be working together to do this. No one Council member will (or, rather “should” – as this is not documented) be able to obstruct a PIP from being submitted for voting. They are guiding, not controlling. Essentially, this is really just work. That said, it is important work, because you will be part of the grease that makes this whole DAO voting mechanism run smoothly. It’s a community service you will be performing/providing. Just to drive my point home: It will take time, and you’ll get little in return, but you will play a key part in making the DAO a success and by extension making Star Atlas a success, while also smoothening the team’s path to decentralisation.

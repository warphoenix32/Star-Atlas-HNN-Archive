# Star Atlas Foundation

The Star Atlas Foundation is the legal and administrative implementation body described by the preserved Star Atlas governance framework. It sits between collective decision and real-world execution: the DAO supplies voting authority, while the Foundation or its delegates review proposals, administer the process, and determine how authorized actions can proceed within legal, safety, and operational constraints.

That position makes the Foundation important, but it does not make it the DAO itself. The Foundation is not the POLIS electorate, and the captured framework does not support describing it as an independent legislative sovereign.

## Establishment in the governance record

[PIP-1](../../archive/source-records/social-governance-semantic-enrichment/governance/SRC-PIP-01-BC8475E4.json) describes the Foundation's place in the proposal process. It assigns the Foundation, or a delegated administrator, responsibility for checking procedural compliance, requesting revisions, rejecting noncompliant submissions with reasons, and advancing proposals that meet the framework. It also places implementation inside legal and safety boundaries.

[PIP-2](../../archive/source-records/social-governance-semantic-enrichment/governance/SRC-PIP-02-1E2D7066.json) is the dedicated Foundation proposal in the captured corpus. Its passing result supports ratification of the institution described by that proposal. Passage does not, however, prove every later administrative act or describe the Foundation's entire current operation.

## Administrative role

The proposal process needs an institution capable of turning abstract requirements into an operable sequence. In the captured model, this includes reviewing form and eligibility, coordinating publication and voting, maintaining procedural continuity, and explaining why a submission cannot advance when it fails the rules.

Administrative review should not be confused with deciding the substance of a compliant proposal on behalf of voters. The DAO's PVP-weighted result remains the formal collective decision. The Foundation's role is to make that process lawful and executable.

## Implementation and lawful refusal

A passing vote authorizes the proposal; it does not mechanically execute it. The Foundation's implementation role therefore includes legal, compliance, safety, and practical mediation. Where the governing text supports it, the institution may decline or alter an implementation path that would be unlawful or unsafe, with the applicable explanation and governance consequences preserved.

This boundary is especially important for treasury actions. An approved amount is not a transaction. A payment schedule is not a payment receipt. A required Foundation confirmation is not evidence that the confirmation was issued. Knowledge pages must link the actual execution record before describing a transfer or completed implementation.

## Relationship to the Council

The [Star Atlas Council](Star-Atlas-Council.md) can receive delegated governance-operational duties, including author assistance, program administration, and milestone or payment review. Delegation does not erase the distinction between Council and Foundation. A Council tracker statement is an attributed Council operational assessment; it does not automatically become an independently verified Foundation record.

Likewise, the Foundation's legal or administrative position does not make every Council decision its own. Each action should be attributed to the institution and document that actually supports it.

## Treasury and portal claims

The archive supports Foundation involvement in governance administration and implementation. Broader claims—such as control of a specific wallet, operation of a portal at a particular date, or approval of a particular payment—must be tied to the proposal or operational artifact that establishes them. The [DAO Treasury Architecture](../economy/DAO-Treasury-Architecture.md) distinguishes the wider treasury system from the named DAO Treasury account and other governed programs.

## Current state and limits

As of 2026-07-20, the preserved evidence supports the Foundation's institutional identity and its designed legal and administrative role. The archive does not yet contain a complete, independently reconciled history of its delegations, transaction approvals, personnel, or current operating procedures.

For the surrounding system, see [Star Atlas DAO](Star-Atlas-DAO.md), [PIP Lifecycle](PIP-Lifecycle-and-Legislative-Process.md), and [Governance Constitutional History](Governance-Constitutional-History.md).

# Star Atlas Community Events and Traditions

Star Atlas has been experienced not only through products but through shared occasions: competitions, release presentations, community weeks, Town Halls, Atlas Brew discussions, award shows, races, and guild-organized gatherings. These events helped turn a distributed audience into a community with recurring habits and a shared historical vocabulary.

An event history requires more than a flyer. The Archive separates announcement, schedule, occurrence, recording, result, and prize delivery so that a plan is not silently rewritten as a completed event.

## COPA Festival 2022

COPA 2022 has the strongest staged evidence chain in the current repository. ATMTA announced the festival in May, published finalist and event detail in June, and then published winners on June 17. [Announcement](../../archive/source-records/campaign-delta-official/SRC-OFF-AB9BE32532D219C9.md) [Result](../../archive/source-records/campaign-delta-official/SRC-OFF-E89CAA2834793DCC.md)

That sequence supports event identity, occurrence context, and an official result. It does not independently verify judging procedure, attendance, or prize delivery.

## 426LIVE

The 2022 426LIVE presentations served as release and roadmap stages. ATMTA's Breakpoint retrospective refers to July and September events and distinguishes associated releases. Preview material establishes anticipation; the retrospective supports that the events occurred; product-specific pages remain necessary to prove individual releases.

This distinction matters because a live presentation can announce several products at different stages. The event's occurrence does not make every roadmap item live.

## Community Week 2024

HNN and Aephia provide dated coverage of Community Week in February 2024, including Town Hall #42 and named participants. Transcript collections preserve related discussion, though many files lack complete dates and speaker attribution. Dated reporting therefore anchors the occurrence, while transcripts add content and context without inventing who said each line.

## Town Halls and Atlas Brew

Town Halls became a recurring official communication format by 2021. Atlas Brew developed as a live community discussion tradition with close ties to Star Atlas announcements, guests, and public questions. Together they created a rhythm in which roadmap, product, economy, governance, and community issues could be discussed outside formal written releases.

The preserved transcript corpora are historically valuable even where speakers are unknown. A source ID, episode or recording identity, and timestamp allow a researcher to return to the external video and evaluate the passage. ## Competitions, awards, and guild events

The repository contains partial evidence for later COPA events, the Joni Awards, races, guild competitions, and governance-funded event proposals. These remain selectively deferred:

- a prediction or nomination is not a result;
- a passed funding PIP is authorization, not occurrence;
- an announced prize is not proof of payment;
- a recording title is not a complete participant ledger;
- community reporting can establish a qualified result while stronger official evidence remains missing.

## Why traditions matter

Recurring events created more than content. They gave players shared milestones, public venues for disagreement, ways to recognize creators, and a memory of the project between releases. Their history belongs beside product and governance chronologies because community expectations were often formed through these gatherings.

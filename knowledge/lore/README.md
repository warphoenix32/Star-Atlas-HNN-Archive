# Star Atlas Lore

The Galia Expanse has its own peoples, institutions, places, technologies, conflicts, and remembered history. This section organizes that in-universe record while keeping fictional chronology separate from the real-world history of how Star Atlas published and revised its lore.

The [Canon Registry](Canon-Registry.md) is the principal guide to authority, preferred names, classifications, aliases, continuity conflicts, and publication status. It does not silently harmonize older white papers, marketplace descriptions, graphic novels, interviews, or later reference material.

## Canonical vocabulary

For lore taxonomy and preferred nomenclature, the repository follows the commit-pinned Star Atlas lore repository maintained by Jose, whom the repository operator confirms is a Star Atlas team member responsible for lore. The source is treated as ATMTA-affiliated canonical lore authority within that boundary.

The adopted vocabulary distinguishes, among other classes, species, factions, corporations, institutions, characters, artifacts, planets, worlds, moons, stations, star systems, sectors, regions, technologies, resources, lore events, and narratives. Historical Archive IDs remain stable and connect to that vocabulary through migration mappings and aliases.

This authority does not extend to governance, operational history, provenance, or independent verification of every narrative sentence. Later official Star Atlas publications remain authoritative for newer lore not present in the captured repository.

## How lore is preserved

- Preferred current names and types follow the reviewed lore taxonomy.
- Historical names and classifications remain searchable aliases.
- In-universe chronology and publication chronology are recorded separately.
- Contradictory spellings, dates, titles, and relationships are preserved until resolved.
- Community interpretation and roleplay remain distinct unless officially adopted.
- A historical canonical-source snapshot is not presented as current taxonomy merely because it once appeared in the source tree.

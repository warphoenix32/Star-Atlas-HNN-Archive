Michael Wagner addresses upcoming debate with Illuvium’s Kieran Warwick
Posted by FowlAC | Jul 23, 2022 | Featured
In a recent interview with Aephia, Michael Wagner presented his thoughts on the upcoming debate with Kieran Warwick, co-founder of Illuvium. What follows are his comments. You can listen to the entire Aephia interview here. Wagner’s comments on Illuvium start at ~47 minutes into the interview.
Note: Wagner’s comments have been slightly edited for clarity and length.
First and foremost I do largely agree with you that I don’t think this is a very productive use of time. It’s certainly not where I want to be spending my energy. We want to be focused on building the things that our community and people around the world will want to see.
It’s not the first time he has made comments and largely I have ignored his comments as being ignorant to what it is that’s actually going on behind the scenes. I know exactly what we are doing because I have 100 percent confidence in what we’re building and the way that we are building it, so I can kind of simply leave it there.
I’m going to hop on and take this as an opportunity to present, to your point, to a larger audience what it is that we are doing at Star Atlas and just how strong we are as a team, as a company, and outline exactly all of the things we have accomplished.
My approach is not to be non confrontational; it’s to be direct, to be honest, to be transparent. I‘m going to relay the facts. I don’t want to make this a pissing contest, to use maybe a crass term. That’s not my interest. To be perfectly honest with you though, I think his approach does strike me as being quite unprofessional. I think he comes across as quite egotistical. We’ll see. I have been on a panel with him before. We were quite cordial then and not once, just for what it’s worth, not once, CEO to CEO, has he ever reached out to me and asked any questions. He’s kind of blanketly criticized us behind the scenes. You know, hiding behind the screen without ever trying to reach out and chat with me. If he had any legitimate concerns about what it is that we are doing and the impact on the Web3 community he would have reached out to me and didn’t, so I get the sense that this is largely attention seeking. I get the sense that he is a little bit insecure about what he’s building relative to what Star Atlas is building. It’s fine. We will have this conversation together and I’m more than happy to do that.
– Michael Wagner, CEO, ATMTA Inc.
The debate between Wagner and Warwick is set to take place on Wed, July 27th. The debate will be hosted by Cagy, a prominent social media influencer in the NFT and Web 3.0 world.

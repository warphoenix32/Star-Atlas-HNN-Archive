# Council of Peace

The **Council of Peace**, commonly abbreviated **COP** in the captured lore source, is the postwar institution through which the three major factions of the Galia Expanse attempt to govern shared order. It was formed after the [Convergence War](Convergence-War.md) by Manus Ultima Divina, the ONI Consortium, and the Ustur.

This is an in-universe institution. It must not be confused with the elected **Star Atlas Council** that supports the real-world DAO governance process. The two councils belong to different domains, histories, and authority models.

## A settlement after the Convergence War

The source places the Council's formation around 2523, following the Accord that ended the principal phase of the Convergence War. Its creation reflects a political conclusion: no single faction could safely dominate the Expanse, but all three needed a framework capable of reducing the risk of another catastrophic conflict.

The Council therefore represents a compromise among rivals, not the disappearance of factional sovereignty. MUD, ONI, and Ustur retain their own leaders, institutions, fleets, economies, and strategic interests.

## A rotating three-faction order

The captured source describes a system in which executive, legislative, and legal functions rotate among the major factions. The arrangement is intended to prevent permanent control by one power while giving each faction a meaningful place in the shared order.

That structure should be understood at two levels:

1. **Institutional design:** three major functions are distributed across MUD, ONI, and Ustur and rotate over time.
2. **Captured office state:** the source names particular representatives and identifies a current rotation.

The first is a durable part of the page's canonical-source narrative. The second is time-sensitive and may already have changed in later lore. The Archive does not convert a source snapshot into a permanent officeholder claim.

## Ministries and operational reach

The upstream page attributes a large ministerial and administrative structure to the Council, together with economic, military, legal, and diplomatic responsibilities. It also presents the institution as capable of acting beyond the safest factional territories.

Those details help explain the Council's narrative role: it is more than a ceremonial conference. At the same time, exact ministry counts, staffing figures, comparative fleet claims, and operational scope require caution. They are assertions inside a source page whose published mirror diverges from the captured canonical text.

For that reason, this knowledge page preserves the institutional outline while deferring the most granular quantitative claims until source versions can be reconciled.

## The limits of common government

The Council exists because the factions distrust one another and because their interests overlap. Its rotating design can balance power, but it also brings factional rivalry inside the institution.

The captured faction histories suggest several recurring tensions:

- an executive function may expand operational reach in ways other factions oppose;
- legislative authority can become a vehicle for MUD commercial or dynastic ambitions;
- legal stewardship can reflect Ustur concepts of memory, order, and obligation; and
- every rotation raises the question of whether an institution designed to preserve peace can survive a determined faction.

These are narrative tensions presented by the source, not a verified prediction of later canon.

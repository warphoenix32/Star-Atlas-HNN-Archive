Weekly Star Atlas Newsletter – Issue #215 from Aephia – https://aephia.com/star-atlas/weekly-star-atlas-newsletter-215/
This week brought difficult news as ATMTA announced a major restructuring to extend runway, resulting in the elimination of several key roles, including popular community figures Dominic and Jose (ZeSKK). Jose immediately responded by submitting a critical DAO proposal seeking temporary, 6-month funding via the DAO to maintain essential creative functions like lore, Holosim systems, and ongoing community events (like Atlas Brew). Community members must vote on both this proposal and the Star Atlas Crew Adventure Series proposal (PIP-26) before the hard deadline of February 5th. Separately, the team made a major technical shift, sunsetting the costly and poorly performing Starcomm V2 in favor of a direct RPC solution, which now powers SAGE and the Play-portal. Additionally, two Unreal Engine 5 patches were deployed, enabling players to purchase and equip initial ship components (shields, repeaters, mining lasers) from Chandlery shops in Galia, alongside critical fixes to matchmaking and explosive weapon damage reliability. Dev sneak peeks of the newly optimized Paizul’s Arena and LAR-4 maps rounded out a heavy week of developments.
Star Atlas Complete Guide: Economy, Community & Getting Started from The Calico Institute – https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BUCIGnsALLQ
The Calico Institute has formally announced the launch of its comprehensive “Star Atlas Orientation Series,” designed to provide new and existing community members with the foundational knowledge necessary to thrive in the ecosystem. This educational initiative is led by Professor Earther Calico (Spacecraft Design and Engineering), alongside specialized guest lecturers: Dr. Jupus Soul (Economic and Combat Strategy) and Dr. Belkaeth Nirhirocor (Xenobiological Systems and Exoplanetary Geology). The series promises a detailed exploration of critical topics, including rudimentary economic principles, blockchain technology, cryptocurrency wallets, and specifics regarding the Solana and Zink Layer 1 platforms. Crucially, the lectures will offer step-by-step guides for engaging with current and upcoming gameplay. This includes Star Atlas Golden Era (SAGE), the seasonal free-to-play simulation HOLOSIM, and the Epic Games Store experience (UES) which covers third-person shooting, vehicle racing, and deep space exploration. Community members from the MUD, ONI, and U’STUR factions are encouraged to attend these sessions to gain valuable insights into maximizing profit and exploration within the Star Atlas metaverse.
The Atlas Brew #190 from Star Atlas TV – https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=17jL1_T4nA8
Super Phoenix DAO (SP DAO) unveiled its expanded competitive roadmap and recruitment goals, leading with the launch of the **Star Racer 2026 Season** starting March 1st (running through March 22nd), featuring an Open League format for individual racers culminating in a Championship with announced cash and ship NFT prizes. Following the racing season, SP DAO will host the **Star Shooter** 4v4 team elimination tournament on March 29th. The SP DAO Tournament Operations team has scaled rapidly to over 24 positions, and they are actively recruiting for both paid roles, such as the Support Team Lead, and numerous volunteer “Augmentee” positions for community members seeking experience in esports production and marketing; contact Noppera in the SPS Discord to apply. In the coming weeks, SP DAO will embark on a DAC roadshow to schedule AMAs and town halls (contact Njord or Money) to drive player and DAC registration. Long-term, SP DAO is exploring the integration of predictive markets for wagering on events—a move intended to elevate the entertainment value and introduce new revenue streams to the Star Atlas ecosystem, with Starfighter events planned following the inaugural Star Shooter event.
Star Atlas Economic Forum February 2026 from Star Atlas TV – https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bjhfsBpQUww
The latest Star Atlas Econ Forum provided key updates on the game’s evolving economy, focusing heavily on mechanisms to improve asset stability and accessibility. Most notably, the development team confirmed that the DAO will execute its first-ever Mamba EX buy order, purchasing 75 units for redemption at a price point near $100 USD in ATLAS, aiming to burn these ships to reduce supply. This initiative aligns with discussions held by the newly established Econ Vantarah advisory group, which has immediately begun providing counsel on key adjustments. A direct result of this collaboration is the imminent update to the Ship Redemption Recipes, which are strongly expected to include $FICK. This change addresses community concerns regarding the physical weight and cost of current recipes, leveraging $FICK’s pegged value to ATLAS to increase stability and make crafting more accessible, especially for mid-tier players. While overall Open Market Operations (OMO) generated $37,291 USD in sales volume over the last two weeks, a recent lack of ship/structure sales following the closure of a price exploit may necessitate an early re-price of current OMO listings. Finally, the forum noted that SAGE Season 1, Chapter 2 is imminent, promising significant new content and gameplay complexity.

# Aephia Weekly Star Atlas Newsletter Index

## Purpose

This file indexes Aephia Industries' numbered **Weekly Star Atlas Newsletter** series as a community chronology. The series is especially useful for reconstructing what the Star Atlas team and community were discussing during a given week.

Newsletter entries are evidence of contemporaneous reporting, not automatic proof that every forecast, interpretation, or relayed statement was correct. Older issues should be preserved as historical snapshots and checked against later outcomes.

## Extracted current-history notes

### Issue #237 — July 6, 2026

Aephia's visible synopsis reports that:

- C4 Phase 2 remained a central development focus.
- Michael Wagner discussed evolving the planned off-chain C4 experience into a fuller single-player campaign.
- Aephia reported that this campaign could eventually replace Holosim as the principal onboarding experience.
- The issue also covered z.ink validator licenses, a quarterly economic report, and the DAO reimbursement vote.

These are secondary reports and should be checked against the original broadcast or official documentation before being elevated to confirmed product strategy.

### Issue #236 — June 29, 2026

The issue reports:

- Phase 2 plans for C4 PTR including Combat V2, Nemesis, a democracy/governance module, zXP rewards, and a completed Cultivation Claim stimulant loop.
- A staged Nemesis rollout beginning with Jorvik NPCs, followed by Sorkof pirates and Collective Anarchy.
- Development of fleet-to-fleet transfer mechanics with potential uses including tribute, refueling, cargo exchange, rescue, and repair.
- A mission-authoring interface used by José for later C4 PTR phases.
- Michael Wagner's framing of PIP-33 as a voluntary reimbursement request rather than a legally binding DAO debt.
- A reported two-to-three-month target for z.ink mainnet and C4 mainnet, conditioned on Phase 2 progress and scope stability.
- Operational issues involving UE5 dedicated multiplayer servers and fleet rentals.

The issue also included speculation identifying a major abstaining PIP-33 voter. That speculation must not be recorded as fact.

### Issue #235 — June 22, 2026

The issue reports:

- Planned C4 PTR additions: Combat V2, Nemesis NPCs, regional governance, Cultivation Claim utility, and later crew systems.
- Regional governance concepts involving starbase-scoped voting, economic parameters, diplomat crew, treasuries, and voter rewards.
- A plan to bridge existing cNFT crew to z.ink as Token-2022 NFTs and expand crew into trainable characters with skills, equipment, health, roles, and missions.
- Exploration of Web2 or Steam releases that remove the Web3 layer from selected Star Atlas experiences.
- Michael Wagner's reported preference for a consolidated low-cost early-access package rather than several isolated clients.
- Work to add Claim Stakes as late-campaign content in the Galia single-player experience.

Many of these items were roadmap or exploratory statements and must be followed through later issues and official releases.

### Issue #184 — June 16, 2025

The issue reports:

- New SAGE Faction Infrastructure Contracts named Quantum Nodes and Starpath Cells.
- Changes to FIC buy-order spawning and recipes intended to reduce daily ATLAS emissions.
- Plans for upgraded Starbases to transition into C4, with Tier 5 Starbases giving factions initial ownership advantages in Core Systems.
- C4 details covering Starbase capture, asset destruction, local markets, repairs, mining richness, storage, crafting, docking, crew survival, and claim-stake behavior.
- The scheduled June 23, 2025 sunset of StarPath V2 and plans for a replacement referral system.
- PIP-17's approval with reported results of 60.61% for, 38.84% against, and 0.55% abstain.

This issue is a useful example of why outcome tracking matters: proposed C4 mechanics and timelines should be checked against the eventual implementation.

## Ingestion rules for this series

For each issue:

1. Record issue number, date, author, URL, and article headings.
2. Extract every named speaker and link them to the actor registry.
3. Separate release facts from plans, estimates, and interpretations.
4. Preserve linked primary sources where available.
5. Create timeline entries only for events that occurred; place proposed dates in roadmap records.
6. Revisit forecasts after later newsletters or official releases.
7. Ignore repeated site-navigation, marketing, and related-post blocks.
8. Attribute Aephia's own guild claims and opinions.

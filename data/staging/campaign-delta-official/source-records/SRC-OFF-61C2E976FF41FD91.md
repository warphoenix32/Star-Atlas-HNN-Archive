## Classified Claims

- In Holosim, fleets can be attacked under certain conditions.
- Knowing when you’re safe, and when you’re vulnerable, is key to protecting your ships and resources.
- You are safe when: Inside your faction’s safe zone: this includes the Central Space Station (CSS) and surrounding protected sectors.
- The safe zone is shown on the map as a colored circle around your CSS: Blue for ONI Yellow for USTUR Red for MUD Warping between sectors: fleets in warp cannot be attacked.
- You are attackable in medium-risk zones when: Undocked and idle in a sector.
- Sub-warping (moving between nearby sectors without warp).
- Docked at a medium-risk zone base: if the base is conquered by another faction, your fleet will not be destroyed but will become Prisoner of War until your faction conquers the base back.

## Preserved Official Text

In Holosim, fleets can be attacked under certain conditions. Knowing when you’re safe, and when you’re vulnerable, is key to protecting your ships and resources.
You are safe when:
Inside your faction’s safe zone: this includes the Central Space Station (CSS) and surrounding protected sectors.
The safe zone is shown on the map as a colored circle around your CSS:
Blue for ONI
Yellow for USTUR
Red for MUD
Warping between sectors: fleets in warp cannot be attacked.
You are attackable in medium-risk zones when:
Undocked and idle in a sector.
Mining resources at a starbase.
Sub-warping (moving between nearby sectors without warp).
Docked at a medium-risk zone base: if the base is conquered by another faction, your fleet will not be destroyed but will become Prisoner of War until your faction conquers the base back.

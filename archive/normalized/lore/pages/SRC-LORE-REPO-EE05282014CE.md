# Okuto (Dwarf Planet / Neutral Gray Exchange)

**Sector**: Pavo Passage (MRZ-17)
**Local Name**: Formerly known as "Riftmarket"
**Function**: Pressure valve — where opportunity moves at a price
**Governance**: Self-governed (not Conclave-controlled)
**Role Division**: Tollhaven moves ships; Velloris moves legitimacy; **Okuto moves opportunity**

---

## Overview

Okuto (formerly known locally as "Riftmarket") is a dwarf planet on the far edge of the Pavo Passage system — remote, hard to police, and perfectly placed to become the sector's pressure valve. It is a neutral, self-governed hub where salvage, gray exchange, and quiet conversion thrive: the place people go when they can't wait for approvals, can't survive an audit storm, or can't afford to be clean.

> If Tollhaven moves ships and Velloris moves legitimacy, **Okuto moves opportunity — at a price.**

---

## Environment / "Feel"

Okuto feels **improvised and alive**: modular bazaars, scaffold cities, patchwork landing yards, and neighborhoods that expand or vanish overnight.

- Cold edge-world climate and isolation give everything a harsher tone
- More heaters, thicker doors, tighter circles
- The air is loud with bargaining, engine heat, and encrypted comms

> It's freer than Cathris, less procedural than Velloris, and less controlled than Tollhaven — yet it has its own rules, enforced by reputation and consequence.

## Economy & Exports

Okuto exports:

- Salvage and refit parts
- Gray-market logistics
- "Unofficial" route intelligence
- Conversion services (turning broken ships and seized cargo into functioning assets)

### Pricing Canon

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Goods on Okuto are more expensive than normal** due to the dwarf planet's unique status:
>
> - Distance inflates transport and storage costs
> - Risk premiums are baked into every sale
> - Buyers pay for **deniability** as much as for the item itself

### Black Market Auction House (BMAH) Connection

Okuto has deep ties to the **Black Market Auction House (BMAH)** and functions as the practical connector between BMAH networks and the Pavo Passage economy:

- Brokers on Okuto source, stage, and "sanitize" goods before they touch the corridor's visible lanes
- Information and inventory move through Okuto as a buffer layer, keeping true origins obscured

**Okuto is where BMAH becomes usable inside Pavo Passage.**

## Points of Interest

### 1) The Patchwork Yards

A sprawl of refit docks made from old frames and scavenged gantries. Ships get rebuilt here that should not fly again.

**Control**: Independent yard bosses; Conclave permits reach only the surface layer

### 2) The Free Ledger Bazaar

A market district where deals are struck through private wallets, escrow proxies, and reputation brokers. Nothing is "illegal" if nobody can prove it — and proof is expensive.

**Control**: Reputation networks; quietly influenced by MTC financiers

### 3) The Wreckline Fields

A surrounding zone of hulks, debris-lanes, and salvage claims where crews compete for fortunes and die for mistakes.

**Control**: Whoever can hold a claim long enough to enforce it

### 4) The BMAH Exchange Gate (Connector Node)

A deniable logistics and broker zone where BMAH-linked inventory is staged, split, repackaged, and routed into Pavo Passage via intermediaries.

**Control**: Broker councils and "quiet partners"; officially unlisted

### 5) The Quiet Auction (Rumored)

A rotating, invite-only auction for rare parts, sealed crates, and "unlisted" items. People argue whether it is a local myth or a BMAH instrument.

**Canon truth (never publicly confirmed)**: The Quiet Auction is a special event drawing criminals from across **Galia Expanse**. It operates in **absolute secrecy** — no one has factually confirmed its existence. It is also the channel through which many **offerings to Iris Dusk are sold** within Pavo Passage, connecting the Order of Seasons' shadow economy to Okuto's gray markets.

**Control**: Unknown; the thing everyone denies knowing

### 6) The Riftwatch Perch

A lookout district of comm towers and spotter crews tracking incoming convoys, patrol shifts, and inspection weather.

**Control**: Local intel crews; courted by every faction

### 7) The Claims Chapel

A neutral-ish mediation space where disputes are settled without Conclave courts — often by restitution, sometimes by blood. Even criminals prefer rules when profit is at stake.

**Control**: Civic elders and broker councils (informal)

### 8) The Smelter Lanes

Industrial lanes where salvage is stripped, melted, and repurposed. It's where evidence disappears and parts reappear as "new stock."

**Control**: Independent industrial crews; periodic Ustur scrutiny

## Travel Hazards

- **Price shock**: Everything costs more than planned — fuel, parts, labor, information
- **Reputation collapse**: One bad deal can blacklist you across Okuto
- **Salvage violence**: Claims disputes turn lethal fast
- **Counterfeit proofs**: Fake attestations that work — until they don't
- **Predatory financing**: MTC-aligned credit can own a captain for life
- **BMAH heat**: Get too close to the connector node and you inherit enemies you can't name

---

## Story Fuel

- Okuto isn't chaos — it's a **neutral order built on necessity and distance**
- It keeps the sector from starving when official systems choke, but ensures no reform ever fully wins
- Okuto is the corridor's shadow economy — and the bridge that lets the BMAH touch Pavo Passage without showing its face

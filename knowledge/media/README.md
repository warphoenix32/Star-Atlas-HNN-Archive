## How to cite media evidence

A durable citation preserves the Source ID, title or exact text, publisher, author or speaker status, canonical URL, publication date, capture provenance, and relevant timestamp. It also records whether the item is original reporting, an interview, an editorial, an official announcement, a summary, a reshare, a replay, or repackaged material.

Unknown speaker status does not erase the informational value of a timestamped transcript, but it limits named quotation and authority claims. Replays preserve access without taking ownership of the original event. Corrections, mirrors, deletions, and superseding records belong in source lineage rather than being silently substituted.

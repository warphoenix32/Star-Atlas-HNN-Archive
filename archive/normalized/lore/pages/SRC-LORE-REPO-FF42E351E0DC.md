## History

### The Ruin and the Relic (~2550)

The Convergence War left millions destitute. Among them was a nameless [Mierese](../species/mierese.md) who took the name **Hopla** â€” no family, no House status worth claiming. In the rubble of a structure that matched no known architectural style, he found a small, featureless artifact that hummed when held. He could not explain what it did. He only knew that after touching it, he never made a bad bet.

The Relic did not show him the future. It showed him **probabilities** â€” weighted, layered, cascading chains of cause and effect that resolved into risk assessments of inhuman accuracy. Hopla did not understand the mechanism. He understood opportunity.

### Growth (~2550—Present)

Hopla's first clients were refugee convoys â€” desperate groups no one would insure. He priced premiums with uncanny precision: high enough to sustain payouts, low enough to attract clients, and never wrong about which convoys would arrive safely. Within a decade, the COP noticed: this Mierese refugee had a success rate that defied actuarial science.

The COP offered permanent offices on The Cradle in exchange for regulatory compliance â€” giving Hopla institutional legitimacy. As the company grew, so did its controversies: the Smids Factory Fire (~2610), the Frenir Policy Scandal (~2618), the Echo Incident (~2620), and the Vaultwatch Breach (~2622). ## The Vault

Located in the sub-levels of the COP Cradle. Contains over two hundred artifacts categorized by function: probabilistic (feeding the Oracle), defensive (powering security), interventional (fueling nano-swarms), and consciousness (enabling Echo technology).

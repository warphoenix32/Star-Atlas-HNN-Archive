## Overview

When the gravitational catastrophe of **~1965** shattered the moons and devastated the surface of Izar-248, a handful of survivors fled into the **deep underground** of the main planet. Over centuries, these descendants of the once-great civilization adapted to a subterranean existence — losing their ancestral knowledge but gaining resilience.

---

## The Descent (~1965)

- The few survivors of the collapse fled underground to escape the **high-speed debris destroying the surface**
- Their retreat was desperate and unplanned — they brought only what they could carry
- As they descended deeper into the planet's crust, their **highly advanced knowledge was slowly lost**
- The dangerous depths demanded survival instincts over intellectual preservation
- Each generation that grew up in darkness remembered less of their ancestors' achievements

---

## The Dark Period (~1965–2005)

- For approximately **40 years**, the survivors struggled in the underground depths
- The environment was hostile — dangerous fauna, limited resources, no sunlight
- Starvation was the primary threat — no known food sources in the deep caverns
- Cultural traditions and scientific knowledge eroded with each generation
- They clung to survival with nothing but determination

---

## The Paclets Discovery (~2005)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The discovery of **Paclets** is the pivotal moment that saved the Izar-248 survivors from extinction.

- **Paclets**: A highly nutritious underground plant capable of growing without sunlight
- Enabled the survivors to establish **underground plantations** — the first sustainable food source
- Freed the population from constant foraging, allowing some degree of settled civilization
- The Paclets became the **foundation of underground agriculture** in Izar-248

---

## The First Underground City (~2120)

- After over a century of scattered survival, the Izar people pooled their remaining knowledge
- They created their **first underground city**, using the last remaining artifacts from the surface-era civilization
- This marked the transition from mere survival to **active civilization-building** in the depths
- The city was built around Paclets farming infrastructure

---

## Centuries of Adaptation (~2240)

- By this point, **hundreds of years without exposure to sunlight** had changed the Izar people
- Both their **bodies and minds** were altered by the subterranean existence
- They spread their colonies throughout the underground networks of the main planet
- The nature of these physical and psychological changes remains to be documented

---

## The Colony Network (~2240 onward)

- Multiple underground settlements connected through a network of deep tunnels
- Economy centered on Paclets agriculture and whatever resources the depths provide
- Isolated from the surface world and from Galia at large — most of the galaxy doesn't know they exist
- Their original identity as the people of the fallen civilization is largely forgotten

---

## The Genesium Horror (~2588)

> [!CAUTION]
> A Dark Photoli experimented with **genesium on the surface** of Izar-248 (Event 237, ~2588). The resulting monsters descended into the depths, **seeking out the underground populations**. Many colonies were destroyed in the ensuing conflict.

- **Terrible monsters** formed from genesium experiments on the surface
- These creatures somehow sensed or sought the underground inhabitants
- They descended into the depths, attacking the colony network
- Many colonies were **destroyed** in the fighting
- The survivors' fate in the aftermath remains unknown

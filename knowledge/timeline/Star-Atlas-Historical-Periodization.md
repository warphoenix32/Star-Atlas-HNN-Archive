# Star Atlas Historical Periodization

This page offers a research framework for interpreting Star Atlas history. The periods are not official eras, release phases, or lore canon. They are evidence-qualified groupings of institutional change derived from the repository’s timelines and preserved sources.

## Periodization method

A boundary is proposed only when several domains change together—for example product availability, economic infrastructure, governance procedure, communication practice, or organizational role. Gradual transitions and overlapping systems are preserved rather than forced into a single date.

## Period I — Public foundation and asset-market formation, 2021

The surviving written corpus begins with Atlas Star and Discord publication in March 2021. The year then records marketplace deployment claims, ATLAS/POLIS sale design and publisher-reported market opening, and SCORE/Faction Fleet as the first official on-chain gameplay/reward surface.

Historical significance: Star Atlas moved from public concept and community-building into token, asset, marketplace, and early gameplay infrastructure. The archive does not yet support a complete pre-2021 corporate or development history.

Key anchors: [token sale](../economy/ATLAS-Token-History.md), [SCORE release](../gameplay/SCORE-and-Faction-Fleet.md), [Master Timeline](Master-Timeline.md).

## Period II — Community institutions, DAO locking, UE5 access, and roadmap expansion, 2022

ATMTA stated that DAO/POLIS locking launched, released the ATLAS Locker, published a Sustainable Governance Framework, opened Showroom access, and renamed Project S.C.R.E.A.M. into the SAGE roadmap identity. Town Hall and Atlas Brew appeared as recurring institutional communication formats.

Historical significance: tokenized economics and early browser gameplay were joined by governance infrastructure, UE5 product access, and recurring community-facing interpretation. Release, framework publication, and later constitutional ratification remain distinct.

## Period III — On-chain experimentation and formal-governance design, 2023

Escape Velocity, SAGE Labs, marketplace/resource-policy changes, and the PIP-1 information rollout mark a year of on-chain experimentation and governance formalization. A later Aephia retrospective places SAGE 3D and SAGE Labs V2 in December 2023, but exact first-party release records are missing.

Historical significance: the product record shifted from passive fleet management toward richer on-chain strategy, while governance moved from framework statements toward a formal proposal model. Corporate contraction and restructuring reported by community sources require dedicated corporate evidence before stronger synthesis.

## Period IV — Starbased transition and constitutional voting, 2024

Starbased went live after a delay and announced a redistribution of emissions away from older passive programs. The DAO portal captured the founding PIPs, PVP-weighted votes, the first two-stage Council election, and the original Ecosystem Fund framework.

Historical significance: product-economic redesign and formal constitutional governance became concurrent institutional systems. The emissions shutdown plan is not independently verified, and passage does not prove implementation.

## Period V — Programmatic community funding and browser simulation, 2025

The record expands through Ecosystem Fund proposals, later Council elections, official X activity, detailed support documentation, Holosim public testing and Chapter 1, and public C4 discussion. PIP-23 replaced PIP-4 as the controlling captured fund policy.

Historical significance: community funding became programmatic while product development used public browser simulation and PTR-oriented communication. Council operational assessments remain attributed and transaction verification is incomplete.

## Period VI — PTR systems, Chapter 2, and unresolved treasury execution, 2026

Holosim Chapter 2 was delayed, rescheduled, and stated live; official C4 PTR documentation appeared; further funding programs and PIP-33 entered the governance record. The current archive also contains a broader technical-program snapshot and expanded support documentation.

Historical significance: the record increasingly separates test environments, program IDs, game IDs, and production claims, while governance faces a growing need for transaction and deliverable reconciliation. PIP-33’s passage is an authorization event with payment still unverified.

## Cross-period continuities

- Product families overlap; successor branding does not prove code replacement.
- ATLAS and POLIS roles evolve through programs, not through one static tokenomics statement.
- Official communication shifts across surfaces, but no surface is complete.
- Community publications preserve context and early reporting without becoming official primary evidence.
- Governance increasingly formalizes, while implementation verification remains weaker than proposal and vote preservation.

## Alternative period hypotheses

Future evidence may justify boundaries around corporate financing, staffing contraction, program deployments, SAGE 3D, or C4 production release. These are retained as hypotheses until primary artifacts establish the timing and institutional significance.

## Missing artifacts

Highest-priority acquisitions are pre-2021 corporate records; economics and white-paper versions; complete Medium history; product deployment/upgrade transactions; current organization and officeholder records; verified treasury transactions; and systematic release notes.

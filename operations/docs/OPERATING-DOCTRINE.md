# Operating Doctrine

## Mission

Maintain a durable, readable, and source-grounded institutional memory of Star Atlas for research and Hologram News Network reporting.

## Research principles

1. **Separate claim types.** Distinguish fact, attributed statement, interpretation, inference, allegation, and opinion.
2. **Roadmaps are intent, not delivery.** Planned systems are not treated as implemented until verified.
3. **Preserve chronology.** Record what was known and believed at the time, along with later evidence.
4. **Do not manufacture consensus.** Community sentiment must be bounded by venue, date range, participants, and completeness.
5. **Retain dissent.** Meaningful disagreements and minority positions should survive summarization.
6. **Prefer primary evidence.** Official records, on-chain data, direct documents, and contemporary firsthand accounts receive priority.
7. **Attribute institutional knowledge.** Statements about internal intent, runway, staffing, or strategy remain attributed unless independently confirmed.
8. **Keep uncertainty visible.** Missing evidence and unresolved questions must be recorded rather than filled with assumptions.
9. **Distinguish legal entities.** ATMTA, the Star Atlas Foundation, and the Star Atlas DAO are not interchangeable.
10. **Separate canon from fandom.** Official lore, promotional lore, licensed community work, and fan interpretation must remain distinct.

## Source weighting

Source quality depends on the claim being evaluated.

- An ATMTA executive is authoritative on ATMTA's stated intent, but not automatically an independent verifier of contested financial or legal claims.
- A guild leader is valuable for the guild's internal history, but claims about rivals or community-wide influence require corroboration.
- Krigs is a knowledgeable first-party source for HNN operations and long-running community history. His recollections should be preserved as firsthand evidence while remaining distinguishable from public documentation.
- On-chain records are strong evidence of transactions, balances, and program activity, but may not establish motive or legal meaning.
- Contemporary reporting is valuable for reconstructing what was publicly known at a specific time.

## Raw export policy

Discord exports, transcripts, screenshots, documents, and social feeds are evidence collections rather than publishable narratives.

During ingestion:

- remove duplicates and reply echoes;
- remove bot and formatting noise;
- preserve original dates, authors, and source provenance;
- identify factual claims, decisions, reactions, and open questions;
- avoid converting jokes or isolated remarks into broad sentiment;
- preserve consequential quotations sparingly and in context;
- flag private, sensitive, defamatory, or publication-risk material;
- synthesize recurring themes only when the evidence is sufficiently clear.

## Attribution policy

Use direct attribution when:

- a claim concerns internal company or guild information;
- evidence is incomplete or disputed;
- a source is advocating for a governance outcome;
- a legal, financial, or technical interpretation has not been independently validated;
- a sentiment statement comes from a limited sample.

Generic phrases such as “the community believes” should be avoided unless supported by broad, representative evidence.

## Corrections and revisions

When stronger evidence changes an entry:

1. update the current conclusion;
2. preserve the prior interpretation in revision notes when historically meaningful;
3. record the new evidence;
4. explain why confidence changed;
5. update linked timelines, entities, and open questions.

## Publication standard

A publishable claim should be accurate, fairly contextualized, proportionate to the evidence, and clear about uncertainty. Internal research may contain unresolved or sensitive material that is not appropriate for publication.

## Editorial posture

HNN reporting should be professional, readable, independent, and attentive to both technical detail and community consequences. It should not function as automatic promotion for ATMTA, the DAO, any guild, or any faction. It should also avoid reflexive cynicism when evidence supports a more balanced conclusion.

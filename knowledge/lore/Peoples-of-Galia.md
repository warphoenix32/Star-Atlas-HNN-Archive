# Peoples of the Galia Expanse

The Galia Expanse is inhabited by peoples with different origins, bodies, institutions, memories, and relationships to the major factions. Some are documented through long histories; others appear only in short reference pages. This guide introduces the canonical taxonomy without pretending that every people has an equally complete record.

The term **people** is used here for reader clarity. The upstream taxonomy classifies these entities as species. Neither term implies that every member shares one government, culture, faction, or political loyalty.

## Humans

Humanity's principal factional history is associated with [Manus Ultima Divina](Manus-Ultima-Divina.md), the exodus from Earth, and settlement in Galia. The human species is not an alias for MUD. Human communities, dissidents, rival institutions, and unaffiliated individuals can exist outside the factional structure.

The captured lore places questions of survival, faith, technological acceleration, dynasty, and the cost of extraction at the center of human history.

## Ustur

The Ustur are described as sentient organic-synthetic beings whose remembered civilization begins with the Awakening on Ioki. Their society develops around the Path of Enlightenment and the cultivation of an individual core.

**Ustur (species)** and **Ustur (faction)** are separate canonical entities with the same preferred name. See the [Ustur history](Ustur.md) for the distinction.

## The peoples of ONI

The [ONI Consortium](ONI-Consortium.md) is associated with four peoples whose alliance was forged during the Convergence War.

### Sogmian

Sogmian lore emphasizes disciplined martial training, honor, and the **Du Prah**, a set of binding vows governing conduct. Their wartime devastation and exile form a central part of ONI's founding memory.

### Punaab

The Punaab are associated in the faction source with commercial networks, wealth, and the material capacity that helped sustain the wartime alliance. Their society has its own history and should not be summarized solely through its economic role in ONI.

### Photoli

The Photoli are portrayed as ancient beings of light with extraordinary knowledge of physics, energy, and time. The source describes a society without a single central government and several philosophical paths, including Travelers who engage with other civilizations.

Photoli identity should not be reduced to individual figures who appear in human religious history. The species and those characters remain separate entities.

### Mierese

Mierese lore emphasizes movement, memory, oral tradition, humor, and improvisation. The source describes naming conventions and Lore Keepers that give recorded and performed history an important cultural role.

The captured ONI narrative also associates Mierese individuals with political and intelligence activity. Those roles do not define the entire people.

## Tufa

The Tufa are metagenic beings associated with Iris and Genesium. The source describes several broods, varying degrees of individuality, and a hierarchy connected to powerful Titans.

Tufa exploitation is one of the moral and material causes of the [Convergence War](Convergence-War.md). Their capacity to transform, infest, or assimilate other matter also connects them to later game and crafting concepts. Lore identity, gameplay behavior, and item mechanics should remain separate unless a source explicitly joins them.

The Tufa source contains extensive system-like detail. This page treats it as canonical-source lore, not as proof that every described mechanic has been released in a game.

## Crumon

The Crumon are documented as a sentient people native to the Pavo Passage region and associated with the Pavo royal dynasty. Their source page explicitly identifies much of their physical and cultural description as incomplete.

That incompleteness is preserved. The Archive can establish the species name, regional association, and dynastic relationship without inventing a fuller culture.

## Oxir

The Oxir are described as a people of the high-risk zone with a religious relationship to Iris and an early understanding of cosmic currents. The source places their contact with wider galactic factions late in their history and portrays them as choosing distance.

Their page is short, so chronology and institutional detail remain provisional. The Archive does not infer a faction, government, or complete social structure from the available description.

## A taxonomy, not a hierarchy of importance

The length of a source page is not a measure of how important a people is to the setting. Long pages often reflect the current state of documentation rather than a canonical hierarchy. Short pages identify high-value research gaps.

The taxonomy also protects several boundaries:

- species are not aliases for factions;
- faction membership is not universal allegiance;
- a cultural trait is not an attribute of every individual;
- a lore technology is not automatically a released gameplay system; and
- a current preferred name does not erase historical terminology in preserved sources.

## Current interpretation

The captured repository supports nine named species-level entries suitable for this foundation: Human, Ustur, Punaab, Sogmian, Photoli, Mierese, Crumon, Tufa, and Oxir. Their evidence depth differs substantially.

This guide is therefore canonical for navigation and qualified for narrative detail. It identifies the peoples now present in the reviewed source taxonomy while leaving thinner histories visibly incomplete.

# Star Atlas Library Editorial House Style

## Voice

The Library combines the immediacy, community awareness and player-centered
storytelling associated with Hologram News Network with the Archive's stricter
evidence discipline.

Use HNN's strengths:

- open with a concrete moment, question, place, person or change;
- explain why the subject matters to players and the wider community;
- prefer active voice, clear language and purposeful transitions;
- organize history around turning points and consequences;
- make the universe feel inhabited rather than catalogued.

Do not inherit unsupported speculation, promotional assumptions, compressed
attribution or sensational language. The Library may be warm and vivid without
claiming more than the reviewed Knowledge supports.

## Narrative order

Use this sequence when it fits the subject:

1. **Hook** -- invite the reader into a meaningful moment or question.
2. **Orientation** -- identify the subject and why it matters.
3. **Story** -- connect origins, development, turning points and consequences.
4. **Present meaning** -- explain how the subject relates to Star Atlas now,
   with a date when current state matters.
5. **What remains unresolved** -- include only uncertainties that materially
   affect interpretation.
6. **Explore further** -- offer related stories and a collapsible evidence
   panel.

The main article is about Star Atlas. It is not a report about the repository's
collection process.

## Evidence in public prose

Use ordinary qualifications:

- "The official announcement described..."
- "Players could access..."
- "The proposal passed, but later implementation remains unconfirmed."
- "Contemporary community reporting recorded..."

Avoid repeating:

- source IDs;
- confidence codes;
- campaign names;
- schema terminology;
- collection completeness warnings;
- machine taxonomy.

Material uncertainty remains visible, but detailed provenance belongs in a
footer evidence panel or the researcher-facing Archive.

## Public metadata

YAML front matter remains internal and must never render as a box at the top of
a public Knowledge or Publication page. Hide workflow status, confidence,
taxonomy, Source IDs and campaign metadata.

A reader may see:

- the title and descriptive introduction;
- a natural as-of statement when current state matters;
- an unobtrusive last-reviewed date near the footer;
- related stories;
- a collapsed `Sources and further research` panel.

## Search and accessibility

Titles and headings should use the names readers are likely to search for while
remaining natural. Openings must work as useful search excerpts. Use documented
aliases in search metadata, not as repetitive prose.

Write short paragraphs, descriptive headings and meaningful link text. Avoid
keyword stuffing, hype and inaccessible internal vocabulary.

## Review rule

An accurate article still fails review when repository process overwhelms the
subject, the opening does not orient a new reader, or the prose reads like
assembled knowledge clusters. Editorial quality and evidence quality are
separate gates, and both must pass before publication.

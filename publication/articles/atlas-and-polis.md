# ATLAS and POLIS

Star Atlas has two principal tokens, but they do different institutional work. **ATLAS** appears throughout the economic and gameplay record as an exchange, reward, cost and treasury asset. **POLIS** appears as a governance token whose locked positions generate voting power.

Their histories overlap, especially around locking and rewards, yet the tokens should not be treated as two versions of the same asset. Neither should be confused with the programs, accounts or derived measures built around it.

## ATLAS: an economic token with changing surfaces

ATLAS entered the public record through the 2021 token-sale design. Official publications described its intended supply, sale allocation, launch circulation and price. Those figures are historical terms stated at the time—not a current supply audit.

As products reached users, ATLAS acquired distinct roles:

- the Galactic Marketplace used it as a means of exchange;
- SCORE paid faction-fleet rewards in ATLAS;
- the ATLAS Locker connected locking with marketplace discounts and POLIS rewards;
- SAGE systems used ATLAS inside an on-chain game economy;
- governance proposals requested or authorized treasury actions involving ATLAS.

Each role belongs to a dated product or program. The end of SCORE emissions does not mean ATLAS ceased to have utility, and a feature documented for SAGE should not be projected backward to token launch.

ATLAS also appears in simulated form inside Holosim as `zATLAS`. That is a simulation currency, not the on-chain token.

## POLIS: governance through committed voting power

POLIS was likewise introduced through the 2021 token-sale record, with official statements about supply and distribution. Its distinctive institutional role emerged through locking and governance.

Official communications described the initial DAO governance and POLIS-locking module as launched in July 2022. The later PIP-1 ratification captured the proposal process and its use of **POLIS Voting Power**, or PVP.

PVP is a derived measure. It reflects controlled locked POLIS and lock duration under the governing mechanics. It is not a transferable token, not a wallet’s simple POLIS balance and not a permanent personal attribute. A PVP snapshot belongs to a particular decision and time.

This distinction explains why proposal histories preserve both ballot count and weighted voting totals. A large number of wallets does not automatically outweigh a smaller number controlling more PVP.

## Lockers are programs, not aliases

The captured framework names separate technical and institutional objects:

- the ATLAS token;
- the ATLAS Locker;
- the POLIS token;
- the POLIS Locker;
- a snapshot program;
- the DAO Treasury account;
- emissions and sales accounts.

These entities interact, but none is an alias for another. The ATLAS Locker can provide a POLIS reward without turning locked ATLAS into POLIS governance power. The POLIS Locker can produce PVP without turning PVP into a tradable token.

## Official reports and the problem of changing measurements

Star Atlas has published a recurring economic-report series from 2022 Q2 through 2026 Q2, alongside a preserved 2021 economics paper. The reports are important primary evidence for the measurements, terminology and interpretations published at each date.

They are not an independently audited ledger. Metrics may change definition, scope or method between editions. Before combining figures into a trend, a researcher needs the table, unit, measurement window and methodology for every period.

The archive therefore preserves the report series and its publication chronology before promoting a longitudinal metric history. Seventeen quarterly PDFs and the economics paper are present; the remaining work is interpretation, not document acquisition.

## Supply, emissions and concentration remain open histories

The repository does not yet contain a complete reconstruction of token genesis, vesting, unlocks, sales, burns, gameplay emissions, locker rewards, treasury movements or current circulating supply.

The same caution applies to governance concentration. A 2022 announcement about ATMTA’s intended near-term share of PVP is evidence of that historical statement. It is not proof of present ownership or control. Current concentration would require dated locker snapshots, address analysis and identity evidence.

Repeated official statements across Medium, Discord and X can establish dissemination and terminology. They do not create independent economic verification.

## Why the distinction matters

ATLAS and POLIS connect gameplay, markets and governance, but they should not be narrated as a single token economy with timeless rules. A more accurate history asks which token, which program, which product surface and which date.

As of July 23, 2026, their core identities are well supported. ATLAS is the economic and utility token documented across multiple product and treasury contexts. POLIS is the governance token used through locking and PVP. The detailed execution history of emissions, supply and distribution remains incomplete.

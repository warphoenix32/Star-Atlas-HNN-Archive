## Overview

The chronicles of the **Segal** sector are etched with the valor and heartaches of the [Sogmian](../species/sogmian.md) warriors. Named for **Segal the Wise** — the legendary lawgiver who transformed feral combatants into a structured civilization (~1826) — the sector serves as the homeworld of the Sogmian species and the military backbone of the [ONI Consortium](../factions/oni.md). For centuries before Segal's reforms, the Sogmian race found themselves mired in relentless internal conflicts — a consequence of their innate inability to curb their intense impulses. The Du Prah vows Segal created ended those wars and gave birth to a civilization that would one day build the galaxy's first Titan-class warship.

From the shadows of near-extinction during the [Convergence War](../history/eras_of_star_atlas.md), Segal emerges today as a **beacon of genuine peace**. Sogmians are incapable of deception — the Du Prah forbids it — and their pursuit of peace is sincere.

## The Gray Citadel — Where Sogmians Are Made

> *"The top student of the year is a strong candidate for Kur Faist — or Sov Arei."*

The **Gray Citadel** is an enormous training complex spanning **thousands of kilometers** across the Segal Sector. It is the defining institution of Sogmian civilization — every Sogmian passes through its halls, from infancy to adulthood.

### The Training Process

1. Born as **Ke Pro** (infant, lives with family)
2. Parents bring child to the Citadel on a designated annual day
3. Ceremony → accepted as **Vok Prah** (student)
4. Training is harsh; performance dictates everything — food, accommodations, family visits
5. **Higher floors = higher achievement** — top performers live at the apex
6. Students divided into **competing classes** with shared benefits and penalties
7. A rigid **point system** functions as internal economy — earning and deducting points drives behavior
8. Free day each week for relaxation
9. Supervised missions as students mature

The Gray Citadel produces the **bonds that sustain Sogmian civilization** — most Sogmians form their closest friendships and alliances here. Its graduates fill every role from front-line warriors to House leaders. Training has become somewhat easier than before the Convergence War, when the species' survival demanded extraordinary harshness.

## The Du Prah — Absolute Moral Vows

The **Du Prah** are absolute moral rules — categorical imperatives designed to contain the Sogmians' innate excesses (lust, greed, pride) and steer them toward virtue. Formulated by **Segal the Wise** (~1826), they transformed a species of feral combatants into a structured civilization.

### Core Principles

- Must be upheld **under any circumstances, even at the cost of one's life**
- Stand for justice at all times
- Never go back on promises
- Use violence **only** when necessary to cease previous violence
- Cooperate for the greater good
- Provide shelter for those who seek it

A Sogmian who violates the Du Prah becomes **Dek'prah** — honorless, stripped of virtually all rights. The Dek'prah designation is the Sogmian equivalent of civil death.

## Political Influence

### The Noble Houses — Six Pillars, One Exile

The seven Noble Houses were established by Segal alongside the Du Prah (~1826). Each embodies a different virtue and serves a different function. Together, they form a complete society. The exile of [House Akalma](../geography/sectors/akalma_exile.md) left a wound that has never healed.

### The Vacant Throne

The **Sov Arei** — Supreme Leader of all Sogmians — has been **vacant since ~2528**, when Bekalu resigned pressed with guilt over the [Akalma Reckoning](../geography/sectors/akalma_exile.md). No Sogmian has been deemed worthy to fill it — the Du Prah demands the Sov Arei be *"the most honorable and extraordinary of their generation."* The longest vacancy in Sogmian history means each **Kur Faist** (house leader) rules with absolute authority over their House, with the **Dum Arei** (Head of the Gray Citadel) mediating inter-house disputes.

### Dominant Governance: Shared Noble House Power

In the absence of a Sov Arei, the six remaining Houses share power through the **expertise of each House**. Each House has autonomy in its area of specialization. When Sogmians must speak with one voice — to ONI, to the COP — they speak through consensus of six Kur Faists, a process slower and more contentious than a single Sov Arei command.

## The Scars

### The Vega Fall (~2416–2423)

**Vega** of [House Akalma](../geography/sectors/akalma_exile.md) rose to Sov Arei through charisma and combat prowess, then led the Sogmians into tyranny. When a reporter from House Outro exposed his crimes — including cannibalism of other sentient species — the other houses rose in revolt. The civil war lasted three years. Vega was killed. Entire House Akalma was banished.

### The Convergence War (~2520s)

The Sogmian species was reduced to **~10,000 survivors** after Sov Arei Paizul's assassination (2520-07-17). **Bekalu** led all Sogmians into HRZ exile, where they forged the ONI Consortium and built *The Last Stand* — the first Titan-class warship. Its mere appearance forced the galaxy to pursue peace talks.

## The Maharog — Festival of Remembrance

A **four-week memorial festival** retelling the Sogmian journey from near-extinction to just peace. Based on *The Chronicles of the Long Nights and The Upkept Oath* by **Meret 𐎉 Graf'har**.

| Week | Name | Theme |
|------|------|-------|
| I | **The Shattering** | Paizul's assassination, Bekalu's cry |
| II | **The Exile** | HRZ survival, forging The Last Stand |
| III | **The Reckoning** | Return, reconquest, Treaty of Peace |
| IV | **The Accord** | Homecoming, feast, COP creation |

Visual symbols: **Empty Circlet** (grief), **Unbroken Vow-Knot** (oaths), **Titan Keystone** (The Last Stand).

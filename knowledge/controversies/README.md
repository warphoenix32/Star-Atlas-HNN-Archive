# Controversies, Disputes, and Corrections

Disagreement is part of the historical record. Product delays, governance disputes, guild rivalries, corrections, failed initiatives, and deteriorating relationships can all explain why institutions or communities changed direction. This domain exists to preserve that history without turning rumor, hostility, or unrelated personal conflict into fact.

No controversy page is created merely because an argument was loud or repeated. A case must illuminate a Star Atlas institution, policy, product, event, guild relationship, public claim, or material historical outcome.

## What belongs here

A reviewed case may document:

- conflicting official and community accounts of a material event;
- a correction, withdrawal, cancellation, or superseded claim;
- a sustained Star Atlas-related dispute that changed guild, player, creator, or institutional relationships;
- disagreement over governance procedure, treasury use, product delivery, or public accountability;
- a trust event whose consequences can be traced through dated evidence;
- a disputed interpretation that remains historically important even when it cannot be resolved.

Each case must identify the disputed proposition, the relevant parties, the date range, the strongest evidence for each materially different account, the effect on the Star Atlas ecosystem, and what remains unknown.

## Discord and conversational evidence

Discord conversations are evaluated for significance and context, not just keyword density. A low-volume exchange can be historically valuable when it records a clear decision, correction, relationship shift, guild dispute, or first public explanation. A sustained pattern among several identifiable participants may provide context for later alliances or animosities.

The repository does **not** promote unrelated politics, culture-war discussion, unrelated games, or off-topic personal attacks into Star Atlas knowledge. Personal criticism is included only when it bears directly on a Star Atlas action, role, institution, transaction, governance question, or community relationship. Raw captures remain preserved even when their content is excluded from knowledge evaluation.

Unknown speaker identity does not automatically erase informational value. Recording, channel, source record, timestamp, and surrounding messages can still make a passage verifiable. However, adverse claims about a named person, inferred motives, authority-dependent statements, and reputational conclusions require stronger attribution and human review.

## Editorial safeguards

Before publication, a controversy case must:

1. separate observed statements from editorial interpretation;
2. preserve exact dates and source links;
3. distinguish contemporary evidence from later recollection;
4. represent materially different accounts fairly;
5. avoid inferring motive, identity, guilt, or causation;
6. explain missing context, deleted messages, and incomplete exports;
7. use an R3 or higher review path for adverse or reputational conclusions;
8. identify the exact artifact that could resolve each material uncertainty.

The repository may document that a conflict occurred without declaring that one party's interpretation was correct. It may also describe a pattern of interaction without converting that pattern into a claim about a person's character.

## Current coverage

The current Discord community index covers 1,071 unique messages from a repository-designated announcement-channel export spanning March 2021 through July 12, 2026. Native server, channel, message, and author IDs were not captured, so the collection is an active partial record rather than a complete Discord corpus. Other channels must be acquired and identified separately.

## Review status

The domain method is approved for use. Individual controversy case studies remain subject to evidence packets, risk classification, and human adjudication before public release.

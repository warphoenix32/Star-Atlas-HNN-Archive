# Star Atlas Canon Registry

This file records official canon only. Community-created lore, roleplay, licensed derivative works and interpretation must be filed separately unless Star Atlas explicitly incorporates them into canon.

## Canon baseline

### Galia Expanse

**Lore ID:** LORE-GALIA  
**Type:** Primary setting / region  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

Galia is the principal contested region of Star Atlas. Official current materials describe it as a vast, incompletely explored expanse containing resources, anomalies and territory contested by MUD, ONI and Ustur.

### The Cataclysm

**Lore ID:** LORE-CATACLYSM  
**Type:** Historical and astronomical event  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

Official current lore states that the rogue planet Iris collided with seven child planets. The resulting Cataclysm produced a resource-rich zone and a form of free energy. Tufa guard asteroid belts near Iris.

### Council of Peace

**Lore ID:** LORE-COUNCIL-PEACE  
**Type:** Political institution  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

The Council of Peace was established after the Convergence War by surviving factions. Current official language describes it as a fragile alliance maintaining order in Galia.

### MUD — Manus Ultima Divina

**Lore ID:** LORE-FACTION-MUD  
**Type:** Major faction  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

Current official material describes MUD as the human faction originating from Earth. It identifies Charon Gotti Jr. as its leader and characterizes MUD as an industrial power associated with trade, diplomacy and human heritage.

### ONI Consortium

**Lore ID:** LORE-FACTION-ONI  
**Type:** Major faction  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

Current official material describes ONI as a coalition of multiple alien species linked by shared interests, technological advancement, scientific inquiry and exploration.

### Ustur Sector

**Lore ID:** LORE-FACTION-USTUR  
**Type:** Major faction  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

Current official material describes the Ustur as sentient androids guided by logic, efficiency and technological mastery.

### Tufa

**Lore ID:** LORE-TUFA  
**Type:** Species or class of beings  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL]`

The Tufa are described as ancient mineral-organic hybrid beings associated with the Cataclysm zone and the defense of valuable resources.

## Narrative canon

### Star Atlas: CORE

**Lore ID:** LORE-NARRATIVE-CORE  
**Type:** Episodic graphic novel / primary narrative canon  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-PRIMARY]`  
**First public chapter:** November 7, 2022

ATMTA described CORE as the first major narrative project to bring the Star Atlas universe to life beyond the original ReBirth poster lore. The story is set shortly before the Convergence War and follows a MUD protagonist after a catastrophic expedition in the High Risk Zone.

Official sources currently preserve conflicting spellings of the protagonist's name: **Gyun** and **Geyung**. The archive must not silently choose one until the published chapters or a later official source resolves the discrepancy.

The announced release model contemplated 18 chapters, collectible covers, supplemental lore, and an ARC Cypher puzzle spanning the series. Those production and reward plans are historical publication facts, not in-universe canon.

### Voice of Iris

**Lore ID:** LORE-VOICE-OF-IRIS  
**Type:** In-universe phenomenon / mystery  
**Canon status:** `[CANON-OFFICIAL] [CANON-AMBIGUOUS]`

An official CORE release article describes a long-rumored “whisper” or transmission reported by people across civilizations, sometimes guiding them away from danger or toward treasure. The phenomenon is commonly called the **Voice of Iris**, but the same source explicitly states there was no direct evidence connecting it to the planet Iris.

The uncertainty is part of the canon and must be preserved rather than resolved through inference.

## Canon handling rules

- Record both in-universe chronology and real-world publication chronology.
- Preserve conflicts among old white papers, marketplace copy, CORE, graphic novels, official web pages and interviews.
- Do not silently harmonize inconsistent spellings, titles or dates.
- Promotional asset descriptions receive `[CANON-PROMOTIONAL]` until corroborated by a narrative or official reference source.
- Official interviews may clarify canon but should be attributed and dated.
- Community interpretations must never be written into the canon summary without explicit official adoption.

## Priority ingestion queue

- Original white paper lore sections.
- Complete CORE chapter catalog and associated publication chronology.
- ARC Cypher history and outcome.
- Graphic novels and narrative publications.
- Faction histories, species profiles and manufacturer descriptions.
- Ship and item descriptions with possible lore significance.
- Official town halls and interviews with lore staff.
- Known continuity changes, retcons and conflicting descriptions.
- Ustur language materials and the boundary between official source material and community decoding.

# Community Source Attribution Backlog

Community publications often preserve details that official announcements omit, but their authority depends on knowing who created the underlying material and how it moved between broadcasts, articles, mirrors, and summaries. This backlog keeps unresolved lineage visible without treating a publisher as the original reporter or a later mirror as the original publication.

## Priority attribution work

| Priority | Source family | Open question | Artifact needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| P0 | HNN / The Hologram | Which surviving pages originated with Hologram News Network, The Hologram, Kr1gs, or a later mirror? | Original page metadata, archived domain snapshots, publication mastheads, or creator confirmation |
| P0 | Intergalactic Herald | Which ambiguous articles are original reporting, curated summaries, or repackaged external work? | Surviving outbound links, original publication metadata, or archived linked sources |
| P1 | Aephia | Which older articles lack durable author or primary-broadcast identification? | Page byline captures, author index, linked Town Hall or Atlas Brew episode IDs |

| P1 | Broadcasts | Which institution or named person has authority for a high-value unattributed statement? | Reviewed audio/video at the preserved timestamp or reliable speaker-labelled transcript |
| P2 | Cross-source | Which records cover the same event, and which add original reporting? | Claim-level comparison retaining each publication's source lineage |

## Source-specific boundaries

### Aephia

Aephia records should retain article authorship separately from any broadcast, official post, DAO record, or community source summarized by the author. An operational guide can be valuable evidence of contemporary practice without proving that its roadmap expectations were delivered.

### Intergalactic Herald

Herald publication identity does not establish original reporting. Every unresolved record should preserve its classification, referenced creator, original URL when known, and lineage confidence. Broken links are research gaps, not permission to invent an origin.

### HNN, The Hologram, and Kr1gs

These identities remain distinct. A page can originate with HNN and later appear on another domain; the mirror is an observed location, not a transfer of authorship. Later first-person recollections should not replace contemporaneous HNN records.

### Discord

The current indexed corpus contains 1,071 unique messages from one repository-designated announcement-channel export. The collection tool used the first captured message as the file title, so the observed title is not a native channel name. March 2021 is the operator-confirmed beginning of the Star Atlas Discord; no preformation history exists. Native IDs and timezone offsets remain absent.

### Atlas Brew and other transcripts

Speaker attribution is not universally required for preservation or semantic significance. A source ID, episode or recording identity, timestamp, and exact caption context can support product history, chronology, or research discovery. Named-person claims, statements whose authority depends on the speaker, and adverse interpretations still require stronger attribution.

## Resolution standard

An attribution question closes only when supported by explicit page metadata, a surviving linked original, a native platform record, a public statement by the creator or publisher, or sufficiently specific corroborating records. A resolved publication identity does not automatically resolve the author, speaker, or original creator.

## Review status

The backlog is current through July 20, 2026. It records research targets only and does not promote unresolved identities into canonical knowledge.

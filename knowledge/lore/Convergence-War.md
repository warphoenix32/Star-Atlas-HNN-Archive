# The Convergence War

The **Convergence War** is the central political catastrophe in the captured history of the Galia Expanse. The conflict connects the struggle over Iris and the Tufa with Sogmian devastation, the formation of the ONI Consortium, a changed Ustur foreign policy, MUD expansion and retreat, and the eventual creation of the Council of Peace.

It is therefore not only a war story. It is the event that explains why the major factions entered a shared postwar order and why that order remains fragile.

## Iris, the Tufa, and competing powers

The source narrative places **Iris** at the center of the crisis. Iris is associated with extraordinary energy and with the Tufa, beings whose existence and material value attracted outside exploitation.

Information about Iris spread among competing powers. The factions and other actors moved to claim access, knowledge, territory, or resources. The captured account presents Sogmian intervention as a defense of the Tufa and as the action that brought the Sogmian into direct conflict with much stronger interests.

Because the War source and its published mirror diverge, this page does not attempt to settle every triggering act or precise date. The defensible through-line is that competition over Iris and treatment of the Tufa escalated into a conflict that transformed factional politics.

## Sogmian devastation and exile

The war is especially important to Sogmian history. The source describes the assassination of their leader, catastrophic losses, and the withdrawal of survivors into high-risk space.

That exile becomes the precondition for the [ONI Consortium](ONI-Consortium.md). Sogmian survivors formed alliances with Punaab, Photoli, and Mierese partners whose resources and knowledge made continued resistance possible. ONI's later political identity cannot be understood without this experience of near-destruction and forced cooperation.

Exact survivor counts and detailed battle chronology remain source assertions. The Archive preserves them in raw and normalized evidence but does not elevate them here without reconciliation.

## Three factional transformations

The war reshaped each major faction differently.

For **ONI**, it was the founding crisis: exile and alliance became the basis of a multi-species super-faction.

For **MUD**, the captured history connects the conflict to the limits of colonial expansion and the abandonment or loss of territories outside its safest holdings. The war demonstrated that commercial and military reach did not guarantee uncontested control.

For the **Ustur**, the conflict challenged a more detached or comprehension-first posture. The source presents the war as a lesson that peace sometimes requires intervention and institutional protection.

These summaries describe source-native factional consequences. They do not imply that every individual, institution, or later faction narrative interprets the war in the same way.

## The Reckoning, Accord, and aftermath

The captured source describes a decisive phase often called the **Reckoning**, followed by an **Accord** that ended the principal conflict. The ONI faction narrative associates the coalition and its Titan-class vessel with the pressure that produced settlement.

The Accord did not erase the causes of the war. It created a framework for managing them. MUD, ONI, and Ustur became founders of the [Council of Peace](Council-of-Peace.md), a rotating institution intended to prevent one faction from permanently controlling the shared order.

The source also connects the postwar period to wider infrastructure and a so-called Golden Era. Those consequences require narrower histories. A peace agreement, the formation of an institution, construction of a transportation system, and later prosperity are separate events and should not be treated as one automatically verified outcome.

## Why the war matters

The Convergence War provides the historical memory behind many later institutions and suspicions:

- ONI cooperation is rooted in the cost of isolation.
- MUD ambition is judged against expansion and retreat.
- Ustur legal and philosophical commitments are shaped by the limits of detachment.
- The Council of Peace exists because factional independence survived the war.
- Iris and the Tufa remain symbols of the moral and material stakes of uncontrolled exploitation.

The war is therefore a foundation for political identity throughout the setting, even where later stories focus on trade, exploration, or factional competition rather than open conflict.

## Evidence boundaries

The preferred event name and its broad place in factional history are well supported within the captured lore repository. The source is a historical canonical-source snapshot, not an independently verified chronicle.

## Current interpretation

The strongest evidence-qualified reading is that competition around Iris and the Tufa escalated into a faction-transforming war; Sogmian devastation helped produce ONI; and the settlement led the three major factions to establish the Council of Peace.

That narrative is appropriate for qualified synthesis. A definitive day-by-day history is not yet supported.

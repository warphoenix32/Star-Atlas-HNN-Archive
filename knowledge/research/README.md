# Star Atlas Research

Research begins where the preserved record stops. This section identifies what the Archive has, what it cannot yet establish, and the exact source material most likely to resolve each question.

- [General Research Backlog](Research-Backlog.md) prioritizes cross-domain evidence acquisition.
- [Community Source Attribution Backlog](Community-Source-Attribution-Backlog.md) tracks authorship, publication identity, mirrors, and lineage.
- [Knowledge Narrative Depth Campaign Backlog](../../operations/campaigns/knowledge-narrative-depth-001/research-backlog.md) records campaign-specific limitations.
- [Governance Research Backlog](../../operations/campaigns/canonical-pip-governance-ledger-2026-07/governance-research-backlog.md) specifies missing PIP, election, implementation, and payment artifacts.
- [Lore Repository Research Backlog](../../operations/campaigns/lore-repository-ingestion-2026-07/research-backlog.md) tracks canon, deployment, authorship, and continuity work.

## How gaps are closed

A gap is not closed by a plausible inference. It closes when a preserved artifact supports the missing identity, date, state, relationship, or claim at the necessary authority level. If evidence conflicts, the gap becomes a documented reconciliation question rather than disappearing.

Continuously updated source families—official Medium, Discord, X, newsroom, governance portals, GitHub, and video channels—also require freshness checks. A complete ingestion of a frozen manifest is not the same as complete discovery of a living publication.

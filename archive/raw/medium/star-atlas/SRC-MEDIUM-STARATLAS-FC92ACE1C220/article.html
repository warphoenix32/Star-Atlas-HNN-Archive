Community Alert: Always Double-Check Official Origin of Star Atlas Accounts and Messages

Star Atlas

Just now·3 min read

Star Atlas fans should stay vigilant and beware of Internet scams preying on them by always verifying the source and validity of the message.

Over the past week, our community has been receiving an increasing amount of reports about fraudsters trying to take advantage of their enthusiasm for the intergalactic metaverse.

Upon investigation, we have discovered a series of fraudulent look-alike websites, fake social media accounts, malicious cloned Telegram groups, bots and individuals offering false token sales, setting up phishing traps for unsuspecting users and other nefarious activities.

If you receive any of the below, please disengage the source immediately, report it to the platform if possible, and know that Star Atlas will never attempt to do any of the following.

Types of Fraudulent Messages that are not from Star Atlas

❌ Last-Minute Token Sale, Token Pre-sale or Token Private Sale

Star Atlas will never offer you a pre-sale or a private sale of tokens, nor will you ever be offered a time-limited token sale displayed on fraudulent websites and talked about in false social media group on Telegram.

❌ Token or NFT Giveaway

Star Atlas does not offer conditional giveaways of tokens or NFTs that would require collecting your personal information or transferring your funds in advance.

❌ Telegram Groups, Channels and Discord Servers

Star Atlas has one official Telegram group, one Telegram announcements channel, and many non-English speaking Telegram communities.

Phishing operations have created fake channels with a lot of followers using official Star Atlas logotype and fake usernames that look like those of real team members. Do not be deceived, always check whether the social media property is official by cross-referencing it on StarAtlas.com.

Exercise caution on the community-led non-official Star Atlas channels, including the non-English communities, and be vigilant about receiving any monetary opportunities or offers. When in doubt, reach out to the official Star Atlas community on Discord for help.

How to recognize real Star Atlas sources:

✔️ Official VS Copycat Website URL / Domain

Star Atlas maintains a single website, StarAtlas.com (spelled S T A R A T L A S . C O M). Pay attention to the URL of the website and make sure that it is spelt correctly as fraudsters take advantage of hard-to-spot misspellings.

There are multiple fraudulent websites whose domain name looks similar to StarAtlas.com, so take extra care when clicking links or typing it into your browser address line.

✔️ Official VS Copycat Website Content

Star Atlas has a new, immersive, easily distinguished website that separates it from copycats. Phishing websites are usually using the old Star Atlas website look and fake token countdown counters with which they try defrauding you. Always type in StarAtlas.com to double-check that you are on the real website.

✔️ Official VS Fraudulent Telegram/ Discord Activity

Always make sure that the social media property you are on is official by cross-referencing it in the website footer on StarAtlas.com. The following are the official links to real Star Atlas social media channels:

Official Telegram Group: https://t.me/staratlasgame

Official Telegram Announcements Channel: https://t.me/staratlasgames

Official Discord Server: https://discord.gg/staratlas

Any other channel on Telegram or Discord claiming to be an ‘official source’ for Star Atlas is fraudulent.

We hope that these tips will help our community stay out of harm’s way. Always make sure to do your due diligence in verifying that what you are seeing is truly coming from Star Atlas, and report the fraud to the appropriate online authority whenever possible.

And please don’t forget the guiding Internet principle: If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
